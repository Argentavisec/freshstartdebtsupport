Why Bank Overdraft Debt Spirals (and How to Slow It Down)

Overdrafts are sold as flexible buffers, but repeated fees and interest can trap you below zero. Each month’s charges reduce your next month’s spending power, making it harder to keep up with essentials and nudging you toward more borrowing—cards, catalogues, payday loans or loans.

  • 🔄 Rolling charges – interest and fees eat into your income every month.
  • 💳 Stacked debts – cards, catalogues, and BNPL add more pressure.
  • ⚡ Essentials at risk – falling behind on utility bills or council tax creates bigger problems.
  • 📞 Collection contact – calls and letters increase stress and mistakes.

Our first step is to stabilise your situation: reduce contact pressure, protect priority bills, and build a monthly plan you can actually keep.

Solutions That Can Include Bank Overdraft Debt

Bringing all your non-priority debts—overdrafts, credit cards, catalogues and loans—into one payment often works best. It reduces charges, cuts admin and adds predictability.

  • Debt Management Plan (DMP) – informal and flexible; many creditors agree to freeze or reduce interest/charges.
  • Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA) – formal and legally binding; one affordable monthly payment and, at completion, remaining eligible unsecured debt may be written off.
  • Debt Relief Order (DRO) – for low income/low assets; debts are frozen for 12 months and then written off if your situation doesn’t improve.

Not sure which fits? Read more about DMPs, IVAs and DROs, or compare them all at Compare Solutions.

Priority vs Non-Priority: Where Overdrafts Fit (and Why Council Tax Debt Comes First)

Overdrafts are normally non-priority. That means you should first protect essential living costs and priority bills such as council tax debt, rent/mortgage, and utilities. Once essentials are safe, you can share a fair, affordable amount across non-priority debts via a structured plan.

  • 🏠 Keep the roof over your head—rent or mortgage first.
  • ⚡ Keep energy and water on—utilities next.
  • 🏛️ Avoid escalation—deal with council tax promptly.
  • 🧮 Use the Debt Calculator to set a sustainable offer to overdraft and other non-priority creditors.

This approach reduces risk and helps you stay in control month to month.

Can Bank Overdraft Debt Be Written Off?

It can be included in formal solutions. For example, in an IVA, remaining eligible unsecured debt may be written off at successful completion. In a DRO, qualifying debts are frozen for 12 months and then written off if circumstances don’t improve.

Write-off is never automatic—it depends on your full picture (income, assets, household costs and total debts). Review the trade-offs at Compare Solutions.

Bank Overdraft Debt FAQs

Can bank overdraft debt be included in a DMP or IVA?

Often, yes. Overdrafts commonly sit alongside cards, catalogues and loans in a DMP or a formal IVA. Which route fits depends on your income, assets and total debts—compare at Compare Solutions.

Will a DMP freeze overdraft interest and charges?

Many creditors freeze or reduce interest/charges in a DMP, but it isn’t guaranteed. A formal IVA is legally binding and usually freezes interest and charges across included debts.

Should I prioritise overdraft repayments over council tax or energy bills?

No. Protect essentials first—council tax and utilities are priorities. Then offer a fair amount to overdraft and other non-priority debts through a plan.

My bank closed my account—what now?

That’s scary, but there are “basic” bank accounts available. Meanwhile, you can still treat the overdrawn balance as part of a wider plan like a DMP or IVA, depending on eligibility.

Can overdraft debt be written off?

Potentially, within a completed IVA or an eligible DRO. It depends on your circumstances.

Collectors are threatening court—what should I do?

Start with your rights at What is debt collection? and practical actions at Stop Debt Collectors. If specific firms are writing to you, see our guides (e.g., Lowell, Moorcroft, Cabot).
