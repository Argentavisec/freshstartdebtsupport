Why Bank Overdraft Debt Spirals (and How to Slow It Down)

How rolling charges keep you below zero

Overdrafts are sold as flexible buffers, but repeated fees and interest can trap you below zero. Each month’s charges reduce your next month’s spending power, making it harder to keep up with essentials and nudging you toward more borrowing—cards, catalogues, payday loans or loans.

  • 🔄 Rolling charges – interest and fees eat into your income every month.
  • 💳 Stacked debts – cards, catalogues, and BNPL add more pressure.
  • ⚡ Essentials at risk – falling behind on utility bills or council tax creates bigger problems.
  • 📞 Collection contact – calls and letters increase stress and mistakes.

Quick wins that often help

Switch day-to-day banking to a basic bank account, cancel any CPAs draining funds, and move non-priority debts into one structured offer. These steps reduce fees, ring-fence essentials, and cut contact pressure.

Solutions That Can Include Bank Overdraft Debt

Bringing all your non-priority debts—overdrafts, credit cards, catalogues and loans—into one payment often works best. It reduces charges, cuts admin and adds predictability.

  • Debt Management Plan (DMP) – informal and flexible; many creditors agree to freeze or reduce interest/charges.
  • Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA) – formal and legally binding; one affordable monthly payment and, at completion, remaining eligible unsecured debt may be written off.
  • Debt Relief Order (DRO) – for low income/low assets; debts are frozen for 12 months and then written off if your situation doesn’t improve.

Where overdrafts fit

Overdrafts are typically non-priority and can sit alongside cards, catalogues and loans in a plan. Priority debts (rent/mortgage, council tax, utilities) must be safeguarded first.

Bank Overdraft Debt FAQs

Can bank overdraft debt be included in a DMP or IVA?

Often, yes. Overdrafts commonly sit alongside cards, catalogues and loans in a DMP or a formal IVA. Which route fits depends on your income, assets and total debts—compare at Compare Solutions.

Will a DMP freeze overdraft interest and charges?

Many creditors freeze or reduce interest/charges in a DMP, but it isn’t guaranteed. A formal IVA is legally binding and usually freezes interest and charges across included debts.

Should I prioritise overdraft repayments over council tax or energy bills?

No. Protect essentials first—council tax and utilities are priorities. Then offer a fair amount to overdraft and other non-priority debts through a plan.

My bank closed my account—what now?

That’s scary, but there are “basic” bank accounts available even if you’ve had a DMP, IVA or DRO. They’re fee-free and designed to ring-fence day-to-day money.

How do I cancel a Continuous Payment Authority (CPA)?

Tell your bank/card provider you withdraw consent before the end of business on the day before payment is due, then notify the firm and keep proof. This helps stop unplanned dips into overdraft.

Can I complain about unaffordable overdraft charges?

If your limit was set too high or increased while you were persistently overdrawn, or charges continued despite hardship, consider an affordability complaint to seek a refund of interest/charges.

What is the bank’s “right of set-off” and can they take my money?

Banks can sometimes move money from one of your accounts to another you owe with the same bank to reduce arrears. If you’re up to date, they shouldn’t do this. Tell them about essential bills and vulnerability, and consider moving income to a basic account elsewhere.

Will an overdraft lead to a CCJ?

Banks can demand repayment and may pass the debt to collectors/solicitors. Court action is possible but avoidable—engage early, share a realistic budget, and propose affordable payments.

Overdraft Interest After the 2020 Rule Changes

EAR vs APR, and why rates look high

Since April 2020, UK banks must price overdrafts using a single annual interest rate (often shown as EAR) with fewer fixed daily/monthly fees. Headline EARs (e.g., ~39.9%) can look high compared with old blended fees. The key is reducing time spent in the overdraft and moving to one affordable plan.

Quick action

  • Use the estimator above to see monthly interest.
  • Open a basic bank account to ring-fence essentials.
  • Roll overdraft + other non-priority debts into one plan.

Right of Set-off: Protecting Your Income

What it is and how to avoid surprises

Right of set-off lets a bank move money between your accounts with the same banking group to cover arrears. To protect priority bills, consider moving wages/benefits to a basic account at a different bank, tell your bank about essential outgoings and vulnerability, and propose a realistic budgeted payment.

Checklist

  • Move income to a separate basic account.
  • Share a fair budget and payment offer.
  • Flag priority bills and vulnerability early.

Breathing Space: Pause Most Interest & Contact

Up to 60 days’ legal protection

Breathing Space (Debt Respite Scheme) can pause most enforcement and contact for up to 60 days while an affordable plan is set. It’s available in England and Wales and can help stop mounting overdraft costs while you reorganise your budget and set one payment.

Who it helps

People with problem debt needing time to stabilise essentials, speak to creditors calmly and build a sustainable plan.

Stop subscriptions dragging you deeper into overdraft

Overdraft Complaints & Refunds

When fees/limits were unaffordable

If your overdraft limit was set too high or increased while you were persistently overdrawn, or fees continued despite hardship, you may have grounds to complain and seek a refund of interest/charges. Build a clear timeline, attach statements and show your budget.

What to include

  • Why the lending/limit was unaffordable.
  • Evidence of hardship and persistent use.
  • What redress you’re asking for (refund of interest/charges; fair adjustment; credit file correction).

Overdrafts & Court: Preventing CCJs

What usually happens before court

Typical steps: default/termination, debt sold or placed with collectors/solicitors, Letter of Claim, then claim issued if no agreement. Acting early with a clear budget and one affordable offer often stops escalation. If you get a Letter of Claim, respond on time with your I&E and an affordable proposal.

Jargon Buster: Overdraft Terms

Understand the small print

  • EAR – effective annual rate; shows true yearly cost with compounding.
  • Right of set-off – bank moves money within the group to cover arrears.
  • Unarranged overdraft – going beyond your limit; now typically priced under a single rate structure.
  • Persistent use – relying on your overdraft as long-term borrowing.

Know the terms, then choose a route that protects essentials and simplifies repayments.

Student & Graduate Overdrafts: What Happens After Uni?

Grace periods, step-downs and switching

Many student/graduate accounts offer tiered interest-free buffers for a limited time, then convert to standard rates. If you’re slipping into persistent use after graduation, protect essentials first and move to one affordable plan. Consider switching before your interest-free period ends if a better graduate account is available, or open a basic bank account to ring-fence income while you repay.

Good to know

If your bank won’t help, you can still build a realistic budget and share an affordable repayment plan across all non-priority debts (including the overdraft).

Switching Banks If You’re Overdrawn (CASS)

Protect essentials first, then switch wisely

The Current Account Switch Service (CASS) can move payments in 7 working days. Some banks may let you switch an overdraft if they approve you. If not, open a basic account elsewhere first to keep wages and bills safe, then repay the old overdraft via your plan.

Checklist

  • Open basic account and move income/priority bills.
  • Share a budgeted offer on the old overdraft.
  • Only apply for a new overdraft if truly needed.
