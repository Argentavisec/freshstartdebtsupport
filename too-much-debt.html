How Much Debt Is Too Much?

Debt is part of modern life — from car finance and credit cards to personal loans and overdrafts. But there comes a point where debt shifts from manageable to unmanageable. So, how do you know when you have too much debt?

Here are some signs your debt may be too high:

  • 📉 You're only able to pay the minimum each month
  • 💳 You're borrowing more just to keep up with current repayments
  • 📞 You're receiving letters or calls from debt collectors or bailiffs
  • 🔁 You're juggling payday loans or using credit cards to pay other debts
  • 🚨 Your stress or anxiety levels are rising due to money worries

If these apply to you, it’s time to seek help. The good news? You don’t have to face this alone — and there are legal ways to stop the pressure and get a fresh start.

Debt Solutions If You Have Too Much Debt

There’s no “one size fits all” when it comes to solving too much debt. That’s why we explain every solution clearly — so you know exactly what’s available and what fits your life best.

IVA – Individual Voluntary Arrangement

If you owe over £6,000 to multiple creditors, an IVA could allow you to make one affordable monthly payment and write off unaffordable debt after 5–6 years. Bailiffs and creditor action are frozen as soon as the IVA is approved.

DRO – Debt Relief Order

If you’re on a low income, have little or no assets, and less than £30,000 of debt, a DRO could freeze all repayments for 12 months. If your situation doesn’t change, the debt is then written off completely.

DMP – Debt Management Plan

This flexible, informal solution helps you repay your debts over time. It’s ideal if you don’t qualify for a formal plan, and interest can often be frozen. There’s no fixed term, and you can adjust payments as needed.

Too Much Debt – Frequently Asked Questions

What should I do if I have too much debt to pay back?

The first step is to stop panicking — then speak to a specialist. We’ll review your situation and explore affordable, legal ways to reduce or even write off your debt. You may qualify for a solution that stops interest, halts creditor contact, and gives you room to breathe.

Can I write off my debt legally?

Yes — through debt solutions like an IVA or a Debt Relief Order. These are government-approved options that can write off debt you can’t afford to repay.

Will my credit score be affected?

Yes — most debt solutions impact your credit file. But this is usually less damaging than missed payments, defaults, CCJs, or bailiff action. And your credit can recover over time.

Can bailiffs visit me if I have too much debt?

Yes — especially for council tax, parking fines, or court debts. But if you contact us in time, we can often stop enforcement through a debt solution. Learn more about your rights here.
