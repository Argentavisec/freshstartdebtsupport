What Is Credit Card Debt?

Credit card debt occurs when you borrow money on a credit card and do not pay back the full balance each month. Instead, interest is charged on the outstanding amount, which can quickly cause your debt to grow if payments are missed or only minimum payments are made.

Common causes of credit card debt include:

  • Unexpected expenses or emergencies
  • Low income or loss of income
  • Overuse of credit cards for everyday expenses
  • High interest rates increasing balances rapidly

Ignoring credit card debt can lead to aggressive collection efforts, damage to your credit rating, and even legal action.

Can Credit Card Companies Take You to Court?

Yes. If your credit card debt remains unpaid for an extended period, your lender or debt collector can apply to the court for a County Court Judgment (CCJ). A CCJ is a legal order to repay your debt and can severely affect your credit score for up to six years.

Following a CCJ, enforcement actions such as wage garnishment, bailiff visits, or freezing of bank accounts can take place. But if you seek help early, you can often avoid these serious consequences.

How Does the Breathing Space Scheme Help With Credit Card Debt?

The Breathing Space scheme provides you with legal protection by pausing most creditor enforcement actions for up to 60 days. This includes stopping interest charges and debt collection calls on your credit card debts during this time.

This temporary relief allows you to get professional advice and explore longer-term debt solutions without pressure.

What Is an IVA and Can It Help My Credit Card Debt?

An Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA) is a legally binding agreement to repay what you can afford over typically 5-6 years, with the rest of your unsecured debts, like credit card balances, written off at the end.

More FAQs About Credit Card Debt

What is the difference between secured and unsecured credit card debt?

Credit card debt is unsecured, meaning the lender does not have collateral. This differs from secured debt like mortgages where property can be repossessed.

Can I include credit card debt in a bankruptcy?

Yes. Bankruptcy can discharge most unsecured debts, including credit card balances.

Will stopping payments on credit cards hurt my credit score?

Yes, missed payments negatively affect your score, but managing the situation through formal solutions is better than ignoring it.

How long does it take to clear credit card debt through an IVA?

Typically 5 to 6 years, after which remaining debt is written off.

Can I still get credit with an IVA or DRO?

These solutions affect your credit rating and may limit your ability to get new credit during the agreement and for some time after.
