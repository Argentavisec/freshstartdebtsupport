Help With Rent Arrears in the UK

Worried about falling behind on rent? You're not alone. Whether you rent from a private landlord, housing association, or local authority, missed payments can quickly lead to stress, eviction threats, and serious financial pressure.

At Fresh Start Debt Support, we help people explore practical options to deal with rent arrears and reduce housing-related debt — always privately and without pressure.

What Happens If You Miss Rent Payments?

Landlords can begin eviction processes after just 8 weeks of arrears in some cases. The consequences may include:

  • Section 8 or Section 21 eviction notices
  • Court proceedings and legal fees
  • Possession orders and bailiff enforcement
  • Wage deductions or benefit deductions
  • Credit damage and risk to future renting

The earlier you act, the more likely you are to avoid court action or enforcement. We can help you understand the steps others take to prevent things from escalating.

Can Rent Arrears Be Included in a Debt Plan?

Yes — if you qualify for a Debt Management Plan, IVA, or DRO, rent arrears may be included alongside your other unsecured debts.

This could help you:

  • Combine multiple debts into one payment
  • Pause enforcement action
  • Freeze interest and stop contact from creditors

Note: We do not offer advice. We help you understand the options others in similar circumstances have used to manage rent arrears.

Can I Get Eviction Stopped?

Once legal notices have been served, time is critical. But if you act quickly, it may still be possible to stop the process — especially if a formal debt solution is started before a possession order is granted.

Every landlord is different, but councils and housing associations often work with tenants who take proactive steps to resolve their arrears.

What If I Have Other Debts Too?

Many people with rent arrears also struggle with:

  • Credit cards
  • Loans and payday borrowing
  • Gas and electricity arrears
  • Council tax debt

We can help you look at the bigger picture — combining debts where possible and reducing your monthly financial stress.
