Compare UK Debt Solutions: IVA, DMP, DRO & Bankruptcy

Struggling with debt? It’s important to compare your options carefully. At Fresh Start Debt Support, we help people across the UK understand the difference between an IVA, DMP, DRO, and bankruptcy, so you can choose what’s right for your situation.

This page compares each debt solution in clear terms, with pros, cons, and who qualifies — so you can make an informed choice with confidence.

Try Our Free Debt Calculator

1. What Is an IVA?

An Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA) is a formal agreement that allows you to pay back a portion of your debt through one affordable monthly payment. After 5–6 years, any remaining unsecured debt is written off.

  • ✅ Stops interest, charges, bailiffs and legal action
  • ✅ Write off unaffordable debt legally
  • ✅ One affordable monthly payment
  • ✅ Suitable for those with over £6,000 in unsecured debt

Learn more about how an IVA works on our IVA page.

2. What Is a DMP?

A Debt Management Plan (DMP) is an informal agreement where you repay your debts over time at a rate you can afford. Interest and charges may be frozen depending on the creditor.

  • ✅ Flexible and based on affordability
  • ✅ No court involvement
  • ⚠️ Not legally binding — creditors can still contact you
  • ⚠️ No debt write-off — all debt must be repaid

Explore if this option suits you on our DMP page.

3. What Is a DRO?

A Debt Relief Order (DRO) is a solution designed for people with low income and little to no assets. It freezes your debts and after 12 months, they are written off — if your situation hasn’t improved.

  • ✅ Low cost and simple process
  • ✅ Debts cleared after 1 year
  • ⚠️ Strict eligibility criteria (low income, under £30/month spare income)
  • ⚠️ Limited asset and vehicle value allowed

Check full criteria on our DRO page.

4. What Is Bankruptcy?

Bankruptcy is a legal process where most of your debts are written off, but it can impact your credit, home, and employment (in some roles). It’s usually a last resort for those with no viable way to repay their debts.

  • ✅ Clears most debts quickly (typically 12 months)
  • ⚠️ High visibility on the public record
  • ⚠️ Risk to your home, vehicle, or business assets
  • ⚠️ May affect job roles (e.g., financial positions)

Need more info? Compare IVA vs Bankruptcy.

5. What Is a Trust Deed?

If you live in Scotland, a Trust Deed is similar to an IVA — offering legal protection, reduced monthly payments, and debt write-off after 4 years. You must have over £5,000 in unsecured debt to qualify.

  • ✅ Only available in Scotland
  • ✅ Debt written off after 4 years (if payments are maintained)
  • ⚠️ Appears on your credit file
  • ⚠️ Assets may be considered
