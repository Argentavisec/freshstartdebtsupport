IVA or DMP — Which Is Better?

If you’re deciding between an IVA or DMP, it usually comes down to how much you owe and whether you need legal protection.

  • Choose an IVA if you have ~£6,000+ unsecured debts, a steady income and need interest and most enforcement legally stopped. Remaining qualifying debt can be written off after completion.
  • Choose a DMP if you want a flexible, informal plan with payments you can adjust. Interest freeze depends on creditor agreement and there’s no automatic write-off.

Rule of thumb: Higher debts + need for certainty → IVA. Variable income or temporary difficulty → DMP.

IVA or DRO — Which Fits Your Situation?

Comparing IVA or DRO hinges on income, assets and total debt.

  • Choose an IVA when you have regular income to contribute monthly and want to protect assets where possible. Typical term 5–6 years.
  • Choose a DRO if you meet strict low income/low asset criteria. Debts are written off after 12 months if your situation doesn’t improve.

Rule of thumb: If you pass DRO criteria, it’s often the quickest/cheapest reset. If not, an IVA may still give strong protection and a clear end-date.

IVA or Bankruptcy — What’s Safer for You?

Choosing between an IVA or Bankruptcy depends on asset risk and how fast you need a reset.

  • Choose an IVA if keeping your home or certain assets is important and you can afford a fair monthly contribution. It’s legally binding and usually freezes interest/enforcement.
  • Choose Bankruptcy for the fastest clean break (normally 1 year to discharge) when repayments aren’t realistic — but be aware of potential asset impacts and restrictions.

Rule of thumb: Need speed and have limited assets → Bankruptcy. Want to protect assets and can pay monthly → IVA.

Costs & Fees — What to Expect

  • IVA / Trust Deed: Nominee & supervisory fees taken from your affordable payments (set out in the proposal).
  • DRO: Government-set fee; debts written off after 12 months if criteria remain met.
  • Bankruptcy: Application/court fees; implications for assets and surplus income.
  • DMP: Provider fees vary; many charities charge no fees. Interest freeze not guaranteed.

Eligibility at a Glance

  • IVA: Usually £6k+ unsecured debt, multiple creditors, steady income.
  • DMP: Flexible; suitable if you can pay something but need it reduced.
  • DRO: Low income/low assets within set limits; debts written off after 12 months.
  • Bankruptcy: For debts you can’t realistically repay; fastest full reset.
  • Trust Deed: Scotland only; similar to an IVA.

Legal Protections & Impact on Your Credit File

  • IVA / Trust Deed / Bankruptcy: Legally binding; stop most enforcement. Recorded on your file for ~6 years (bankruptcy from discharge).
  • DRO: Legal moratorium for 12 months; mark remains for 6 years.
  • DMP: Informal; creditors may resume action; defaults/arrangements can affect your file.

How Long Do They Last?

  • IVA: 5–6 years (sometimes +12 months instead of equity release).
  • DMP: Varies by debt size and affordable payment.
  • DRO: 12 months to write-off (if circumstances fit).
  • Bankruptcy: ~1 year to discharge; some payments/orders may continue longer.
  • Trust Deed: ~4 years in Scotland.

How to Choose the Best Debt Solution (Step-by-Step)

  1. List your debts. Note balance, arrears, and whether each is secured/unsecured.
  2. Prioritise essentials. Keep rent/mortgage, council tax and energy up to date first.
  3. Build a realistic budget. Include food, transport and essentials — not just debts.
  4. Match options. Compare IVA/DMP/DRO/Bankruptcy/Trust Deed against your situation.
  5. Choose and start. Formal options usually freeze interest and stop most enforcement once approved.

Yes, if your circumstances change. Always get advice first to avoid unintended consequences.

Will I lose my home or car?

Formal solutions aim to protect essentials but equity and high-value assets can affect terms. We’ll explain safeguards and options clearly.
