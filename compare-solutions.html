IVA vs Debt Management Plan (DMP)

Both an IVA and a Debt Management Plan involve making affordable monthly payments. The key difference is that an IVA is legally binding and can write off unaffordable debt at the end â€” a DMP cannot.

  • IVA: Legally binding, writes off debt at the end, protects you from legal action
  • DMP: Informal, flexible, but no debt write-off or legal protection

IVA vs Debt Relief Order (DRO)

An IVA is suitable for people with regular income, while a DRO is designed for people with very low income and minimal assets. DROs are easier to qualify for if you meet strict criteria.

  • IVA: Better for people with higher debt and stable income

IVA vs Bankruptcy

Bankruptcy and IVAs both offer legal protection and debt write-off. Bankruptcy is more drastic and may put your assets at risk. An IVA gives more control and may help protect your home.

  • IVA: Controlled plan, keep more assets, less stigma
  • Bankruptcy: Faster write-off, but can involve asset loss and greater impact on credit and career

What About a Trust Deed?

A Trust Deed is a Scottish alternative to an IVA. It offers similar benefits: affordable monthly payments, protection from creditors, and the chance to write off debt after 4 years (instead of 5-6 with an IVA).

If you live in Scotland, a Trust Deed may be the most suitable debt solution available.
