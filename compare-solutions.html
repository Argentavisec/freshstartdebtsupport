Popular Debt Solutions in the UK Explained

Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA)

An IVA is a formal agreement approved by your creditors where you make affordable monthly payments over five or six years. After completion, remaining unsecured debts can be legally written off. IVAs provide legal protection from court actions and bailiffs.

Learn More About IVAs

Debt Management Plan (DMP)

A DMP is an informal, flexible plan to repay your debts with reduced monthly payments. It can freeze interest and charges but does not provide legal protection. Ideal for those who want to avoid formal insolvency and have a regular income.

Discover Debt Management Plans

Debt Relief Order (DRO)

DROs help individuals with low income and minimal assets. Debts are frozen for 12 months, after which qualifying debts are written off. DROs are a low-cost alternative to bankruptcy for people with smaller debts.

Find Out About DROs

Bankruptcy

Bankruptcy is a formal insolvency process offering a fresh start by discharging most debts after a year. It has serious implications for your credit rating and assets, and should be considered as a last resort.

Understand Bankruptcy

Trust Deed (Scotland)

A Trust Deed is a formal, voluntary debt solution in Scotland similar to an IVA. It offers legal protection and debt write-off after typically four years. This is specific to Scottish residents.

Learn About Trust Deeds

Comparing Costs and Fees of Debt Solutions

Cost is a major factor when choosing a debt solution. Here‚Äôs a general comparison:

  • IVA: Setup fees and ongoing monthly fees; typically higher than informal plans but includes legal protection.
  • DMP: Often low or no setup fees; fees paid to debt management companies may vary; no legal protection.
  • DRO: Low application fee (approx. ¬£90); no ongoing fees; legal protection included.
  • Bankruptcy: Court fees and possibly trustee fees; no monthly payments required but impact on assets.
  • Trust Deed: Fees included within payments; legal protection for Scottish residents.

Eligibility Criteria Compared

Different solutions have varying eligibility requirements:

  • IVA: Usually for debts over ¬£6,000 with multiple creditors; requires proof of income and affordability.
  • DMP: Suitable for anyone struggling with repayments; informal with no strict criteria.
  • DRO: For low income individuals with debts under ¬£30,000 and minimal assets.
  • Bankruptcy: No minimum debt amount; available to those who cannot repay debts.
  • Trust Deed: Specific to Scottish residents with multiple debts and regular income.

Legal Protections and Impact on Your Credit Report

Understanding legal protections and credit implications helps you choose wisely:

  • IVA: Legally binding, protects you from enforcement; stays on credit file for 6 years.
  • DMP: Not legally binding; creditors can resume enforcement; may affect credit score.
  • DRO: Legal protection for 12 months; credit record marks for 6 years.
  • Bankruptcy: Full legal protection; serious credit impact; stays on file for 6 years or more.
  • Trust Deed: Legally binding in Scotland; impacts credit for up to 6 years.

How Long Do Debt Solutions Last?

The length of time to repay or discharge debts varies:

  • IVA: Typically 5-6 years.
  • DMP: Varies based on affordability; can be many years.
  • DRO: 12-month debt freeze before write-off.
  • Bankruptcy: Usually 1 year before discharge.
  • Trust Deed: Generally 4 years in Scotland.
