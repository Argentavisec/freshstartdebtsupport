Compare IVA vs DMP vs Bankruptcy

Choosing the right debt solution is important. Below we’ve laid out the key differences between the three most common routes: IVAs, Debt Management Plans (DMPs), and Bankruptcy.

Feature IVA DMP Bankruptcy
Debt Written Off Yes – up to 85% No Yes – all included debts
Legally Binding Yes No Yes
Creditor Protection Yes – creditors must stop contact Not guaranteed Yes – automatic on approval
Duration Usually 5–6 years Until debts are paid off Typically 1 year (restrictions up to 6)
Credit Rating Impact Negative during term Negative while active Severe – long-lasting
Home Protection Yes, equity considered Yes May be affected
Eligibility £6,000+ debt, regular income Any debt, regular payment ability No income or assets needed

Which is Right for You?

If you want to protect your home and write off a portion of your debt without going bankrupt, an IVA may be best. If you prefer an informal route with flexible terms, a DMP may suit. If you have no income and cannot repay debts at all, bankruptcy could be a last resort.
