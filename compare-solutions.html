IVA vs DMP — What’s the Difference?

If you’re deciding between an IVA vs DMP, it usually comes down to how much you owe and whether you need legal protection. IVAs are legally binding, typically freeze interest/enforcement and can write off qualifying balances at completion; DMPs are flexible and informal but rely on creditor agreement for interest freezes.

When an IVA suits better

Higher unsecured debts (~£6k+), steady income, and you want certainty on interest/enforcement being stopped. Remaining qualifying debt can be written off after completion.

When a DMP suits better

Variable income or temporary difficulties where flexibility matters; no automatic write-off and freezes depend on creditor consent.

Is an IVA cheaper than a DMP?

IVA fees are built into approved contributions; many DMPs via charities are fee-free — but interest may continue unless creditors agree.

IVA vs DRO — Which Is Best?

Comparing IVA vs DRO hinges on income, assets and total debt. If you meet strict low income/low asset criteria, a DRO can be the quickest, lowest-cost reset with write-off after 12 months; otherwise, an IVA offers legal protection and a structured route to write-off at completion.

Do I pay fees upfront?

IVA fees are usually taken from contributions after approval. MoneyHelper outlines the DRO process and criteria.

IVA vs Bankruptcy — What Should I Choose?

Choosing between IVA vs Bankruptcy depends on asset risk and how fast you need a reset. Bankruptcy tends to be the quickest (about a year to discharge) but can affect assets and has restrictions; an IVA can help protect assets if you can afford monthly contributions.

Will either option wreck my credit?

Both affect your credit for roughly six years (bankruptcy from discharge). Many people rebuild after completion/discharge with careful budgeting.

Costs & Fees — What to Expect

How fees typically work

  • IVA / Trust Deed: Nominee & supervisory fees set out in the proposal and taken from affordable monthly payments once approved.
  • DRO: Government-administered process for low income/low assets (see latest criteria on MoneyHelper).
  • Bankruptcy: Application/court fees; consider impacts on assets and surplus income.
  • DMP: Many charity DMPs are fee-free; interest freezes are not guaranteed.

Exact amounts and processes can change — we’ll check the latest during your free assessment.

Eligibility at a Glance

Each route has criteria. General themes:

  • IVA: Usually £6k+ unsecured debt, multiple creditors, steady income (assessed by a licensed Insolvency Practitioner).
  • DMP: Flexible if you can pay something but need it reduced; informal and adjustable.
  • DRO: Low income/low assets within set limits; qualifying debts can be written off after 12 months if circumstances don’t improve.
  • Bankruptcy: For debts you can’t realistically repay; fastest full reset with restrictions.
  • Trust Deed: Scotland only; similar to an IVA with its own thresholds.

Legal Protections & Impact on Your Credit File

IVAs/Trust Deeds/Bankruptcy are formal, legally binding solutions that usually stop most enforcement; a DRO adds a legal moratorium for 12 months. Marks typically remain around six years (bankruptcy from discharge). DMPs are informal so creditor action can resume.

How Long Do They Last?

  • IVA: 5–6 years (sometimes +12 months instead of equity release).
  • DMP: Varies by debt size and affordable payment.
  • DRO: Usually 12 months to write-off if circumstances fit.
  • Bankruptcy: ~1 year to discharge; some orders may continue longer.
  • Trust Deed: ~4 years in Scotland.

How to Choose the Best Debt Solution (Step-by-Step)

  1. List your debts. Note balance, arrears, and whether each is secured/unsecured.
  2. Prioritise essentials. Keep rent/mortgage, council tax and energy up to date first.
  3. Build a realistic budget. Include food, transport and essentials — not just debts.
  4. Match options. Compare IVA/DMP/DRO/Bankruptcy/Trust Deed against your situation.
  5. Choose and start. Formal options usually freeze interest and stop most enforcement once approved.
