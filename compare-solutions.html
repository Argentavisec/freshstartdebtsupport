Let’s Break It Down — Debt Solution Comparisons

Not sure which solution suits your needs? Here are some head-to-head comparisons to help make things clearer:

IVA vs DMP

Both allow you to make affordable monthly payments. However, an IVA is legally binding and can write off a large portion of debt at the end, while a DMP requires full repayment over time and is not legally binding.

IVA vs DRO

An IVA suits those with higher debt and regular income, whereas a DRO is aimed at people with very low income, no assets, and under £30,000 in total debt. Both offer legal protection from creditors.

IVA vs Bankruptcy

Bankruptcy may offer faster debt clearance but can risk your assets (like your home). An IVA protects assets and offers more structure — ideal if you can afford some repayment each month.

DMP vs DRO

A DMP is informal and flexible, suitable for people with higher income or assets. A DRO is legally binding, suited for those with minimal income and no valuable assets.

DMP vs Bankruptcy

Bankruptcy wipes debt but may have long-term consequences and affect your home or job. A DMP avoids legal action and lets you repay over time, but you must repay all of the debt in full.

Bankruptcy vs DRO

Both are formal insolvency solutions, but a DRO has tighter qualification rules and no upfront fees. Bankruptcy can cost more but allows for larger debts and broader eligibility.

Trust Deed vs IVA

Available only in Scotland, a Trust Deed is very similar to an IVA. Both write off unaffordable debt, but a Trust Deed typically lasts 48 months, while an IVA runs for around 60 months.
