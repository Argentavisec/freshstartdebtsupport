What Is an IVA Company?

An Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA) is a legally binding agreement to repay debt affordably, usually over 5–6 years. An IVA company (Insolvency Practitioner firm) prepares your proposal, liaises with creditors, manages payments, and supports you through any changes.

2025 IVA Protocol Changes (What Homeowners Should Know)

From 1 July 2025, the revised Consumer IVA Protocol took effect to improve protections and service standards. Two key points for homeowners:

  • 60 vs 72 months: Protocol IVAs are proposed for 60 months, or 72 months where there’s over £10,000 beneficial interest in a family home (calculated at 85% of value minus secured borrowing).
  • Reduced remortgage pressure: Where equity release isn’t suitable, extending the term is used instead of forcing remortgage — helping protect people’s homes.

IVA vs Other Debt Solutions

IVA vs DMP

An IVA is legally binding and can write off remaining debt on completion; a Debt Management Plan is informal and has no guaranteed write-off.

IVA vs DRO

A Debt Relief Order suits low income and debts under ~£30,000. IVAs can suit higher debts with steady income.

IVA vs Bankruptcy

Bankruptcy can be faster but may impact assets more. An IVA can be less disruptive and more flexible, depending on your situation.

IVA Companies — Frequently Asked Questions

Do IVA companies charge fees?

Yes, but fees are agreed with creditors and built into your IVA payments — no upfront charges.

How long does an IVA last?

Typically 5–6 years. If you complete the plan, remaining eligible debt is written off.

What changed in 2025 for homeowners?

The revised Protocol (from 1 July 2025) clarifies that where equity release isn’t suitable, a term extension is used instead of remortgaging, with Protocol IVAs set at 60 months or 72 months if there’s over £10,000 beneficial interest in a family home.

Will an IVA stop calls and bailiffs?

Once approved, an IVA is legally binding and usually stops enforcement and freezes interest/charges.

Can I check my IVA reference number?

Yes. Your IVA firm will provide your IVA reference — keep it for creditor communications.

Which IVA company is best?

The “best” firm is the one that responds quickly, explains trade-offs clearly, and supports you personally — not just the biggest brand.

Are IVA companies regulated?

Insolvency Practitioners (IPs) are authorised by professional bodies (e.g., IPA/ICAEW). Marketing and advice rules also exist — see the FCA and ASA resources linked above.

What should I avoid when choosing?

Avoid upfront fees, pressure to sign quickly, and providers who won’t explain the 2025 homeowner rules in writing.
