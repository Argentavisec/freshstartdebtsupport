What Is an IVA Company?

An Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA) is a legally binding debt solution. An IVA company (or Insolvency Practitioner) helps you:

  • Prepare your IVA proposal
  • Negotiate with creditors
  • Manage payments over 5–6 years
  • Provide support if creditors contact you

Choosing the right IVA firm is crucial for affordable payments and reliable protection from creditors.

Can You Switch IVA Companies?

Yes — before approval it may be simple to switch. After approval it’s more complex but possible. Always seek advice before changing providers.

What Debts Can Be Included in an IVA?

  • Credit cards and loans
  • Overdrafts and payday loans
  • Council tax and utility arrears

Usually excluded: student loans, child maintenance, court fines.

IVA vs Other Debt Solutions

IVA vs DMP

An IVA is binding and can write off debt. A Debt Management Plan is informal.

IVA vs DRO

A DRO suits low income and under £30k debt. IVAs suit higher debts.

IVA vs Bankruptcy

Bankruptcy clears debt faster but may risk assets. IVAs can protect them.

IVA Fees — Explained

All IVA companies charge fees, but they are:

  • Built into monthly IVA payments
  • Agreed with creditors upfront
  • Not paid separately
