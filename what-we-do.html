Compare Debt Solutions – IVA vs DMP vs DRO vs Bankruptcy

Looking for the right way to deal with your debts? Whether you're considering an Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA), a Debt Management Plan (DMP), a Debt Relief Order (DRO), or even bankruptcy, understanding your options is the first step.

Use this guide to compare the most popular UK debt solutions and find out which one suits your situation best.

Quick Comparison: Key Differences at a Glance

Solution Write Off Debt Legal Protection Monthly Payments Duration
IVA Yes Yes Yes 5–6 years
DMP No No Yes Flexible
DRO Yes Yes No 12 months
Bankruptcy Yes Yes Sometimes 12 months

IVA vs Debt Management Plan (DMP)

Both the IVA and the DMP allow you to make affordable monthly payments based on your income. However, an IVA is legally binding and allows for up to 80% of your debt to be written off at the end. A DMP is informal and you repay the full amount unless your creditors agree to freeze interest.

  • ✅ IVA includes legal protection
  • ✅ DMP is easier to enter but may take longer
  • ✅ IVA writes off unaffordable debt after 5 years
  • ✅ DMP gives flexibility to adjust or cancel at any time

IVA vs Debt Relief Order (DRO)

Debt Relief Orders are designed for people with low income, few assets, and debts under £30,000. They offer complete debt write-off after 12 months with no monthly repayments. An IVA suits those with regular income who don’t meet DRO criteria but still need help reducing unaffordable debt.

  • ✅ DROs are for people with very limited income and no home
  • ✅ IVAs allow debt write-off even with higher debt levels
  • ✅ DROs last just 12 months, IVAs usually 5 years
  • ✅ Both offer legal protection from creditors

IVA vs Bankruptcy

Both options legally clear your debt, but they work differently. Bankruptcy can impact your job and assets, while an IVA is more controlled and helps you protect your home and belongings.

  • ✅ Bankruptcy may be faster but more severe
  • ✅ IVA helps you avoid asset loss in most cases
  • ✅ Bankruptcy is court-based; IVA is arranged through a specialist
  • ✅ Both show on your credit file for 6 years

DRO vs DMP

If you qualify, a Debt Relief Order is often quicker and more effective than a Debt Management Plan. A DRO can wipe your debts in 12 months with no payments, while a DMP will require you to repay your debts in full.

  • ✅ DROs are ideal for low income and no assets
  • ✅ DMPs work better if you can afford some repayments
  • ✅ DRO offers debt write-off, DMP does not
  • ✅ Both affect your credit rating

Bankruptcy vs DMP

Bankruptcy is more severe and may involve court and loss of assets, but it clears debt faster. A DMP is informal, flexible, and won’t involve legal action — but it does not write off debt.

  • ✅ Bankruptcy clears most debts in 12 months
  • ✅ DMP is easier to manage and stop if needed
  • ✅ Bankruptcy may impact your job
  • ✅ DMP allows you to repay what you can afford over time

Bankruptcy vs DRO

Both are legal insolvency solutions, but the DRO is for those with debts under £30,000, no assets, and very low income. Bankruptcy applies when debts are higher or assets are involved.

  • ✅ DRO is cheaper and quicker (no court)
  • ✅ Bankruptcy is available regardless of income/assets
  • ✅ Both last 12 months and write off debts
  • ✅ Bankruptcy may have more restrictions and consequences

Trust Deed (Scotland Only)

A Trust Deed is similar to an IVA but only available in Scotland. It allows you to make affordable payments over 4 years, after which unaffordable debt is written off. Creditors can’t take legal action once a Trust Deed is protected.

  • ✅ Debts written off after 4 years
  • ✅ Legal protection from creditors
  • ✅ Only for residents of Scotland
