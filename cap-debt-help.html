Understanding HP, PCP, PCH & Loans: Car Debt Help That Fits Your Agreement

Different agreements require different car debt help. With HP/PCP, the lender usually owns the vehicle until you finish paying; with a personal loan, you own the car and the loan is unsecured; with PCH (lease), it’s a hire arrangement. Knowing the type determines which car debt help tactics work—like time to pay, variations, or VT.

Why this matters

  • HP/PCP: car debt help may include reduced payments, catch-ups or VT considerations.
  • Personal loan: car debt help sits with your general unsecured debts (think DMP or IVA).
  • Lease/PCH: car debt help focuses on returning the vehicle or negotiating terms.

Voluntary Termination (VT): When Car Debt Help Means Stopping the Spiral

For HP/PCP, VT can be part of car debt help if the car is unaffordable. If you’ve paid around half the total amount payable (TAP), you may have the right to end the agreement and return the vehicle (conditions apply). We’ll explain pros/cons, costs, condition expectations, and how VT fits wider car debt help such as a DMP or IVA.

When VT helps

  • Balloon payment is unrealistic—VT plus alternative car debt help may be cleaner.
  • Vehicle no longer essential or too costly to run—VT can reset your budget for broader car debt help.
