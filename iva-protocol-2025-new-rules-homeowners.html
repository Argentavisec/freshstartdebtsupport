IVA Protocol 2025: What’s Changed and Why It Matters if You Own a Home

The IVA Protocol 2025 is a major update to the industry rules that sit behind many Individual Voluntary Arrangements (IVAs) in England & Wales. It changes how protocol-compliant IVAs are set up, how long they last, and — crucially for many people — how your home is treated.

The Insolvency Service has confirmed that the revised Protocol will apply to new protocol IVAs from summer 2025, after a short transition where both the 2021 and 2025 versions could be used. All new protocol IVAs must use the 2025 version going forward, which is designed to improve consumer protection and clamp down on poor IVA practice.

If you’re exploring an IVA now — especially as a homeowner — it’s vital you understand what has changed so you can decide whether the new rules work in your favour, or whether a different route (such as a Debt Management Plan or DRO) might be better. This guide focuses on the new IVA rules 2025 for homeowners, equity and 5–6 year terms, not just IVAs in general.

Quick Recap: What Is an IVA — and What Is the IVA Protocol?

What is an IVA?

An Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA) is a formal agreement with your unsecured creditors. You pay what you can afford each month, usually for 5–6 years. At the end, any remaining qualifying unsecured debt can be written off, as long as you’ve stuck to the terms.

People often search for “IVA to write off debt” or “IVA to stop creditor action”. A well-set up IVA should do both: freeze most interest and charges, and give structured protection while you pay one affordable monthly amount.

What is the IVA Protocol?

The IVA Protocol is a set of agreed industry standards for consumer IVAs. It’s a voluntary framework that IVA providers and creditors can sign up to. Where a “protocol IVA” is used:

  • Your Insolvency Practitioner (IP) agrees to follow standard rules and documents.
  • Creditors agree to treat protocol-compliant proposals in a consistent way.
  • You should get clearer information on risks, alternatives, and what happens if your circumstances change.

Not every IVA follows the Protocol, and not every case can. But if yours does, the 2025 version sets important minimum standards for home equity, term length, reviews and variations.

IVA Protocol 2025 vs older IVA rules

Under the older 2021 version, your home could still be pulled into the arrangement through an equity release clause near the end. Under the new IVA Protocol 2025, the emphasis has shifted firmly to disregarding modest equity and using an extra year of payments instead of remortgaging where equity is higher.

Key IVA Protocol 2025 Changes at a Glance

Headline changes for homeowners under the new IVA rules 2025

Advice organisations and IVA providers have picked out several big changes in the 2025 Protocol — especially for homeowners. In plain English, the main updates include:

  • Higher equity threshold: your share of home equity below £10,000 is effectively ignored (previously around £5,000). In those cases, the IVA is usually proposed for 60 months without extra checks near the end.
  • No forced remortgage in protocol IVAs: under the new rules, protocol IVAs must not require you to release equity from your family home by remortgaging or taking a secured loan just to pay creditors. Instead, the length of the IVA does the work.
  • 5–6 year terms based on your equity: if your share of equity is under £10,000, the IVA term is typically 5 years; if it’s £10,000 or more, the term is usually 6 years (72 months) instead of forcing equity release.
  • The family home is generally excluded from IVA realisations: guidance for professionals now makes it clear that, for protocol IVAs, your family home is not treated as an asset to be sold to repay debts.
  • More flexible responses to life changes: supervisors can usually approve bigger payment reductions (up to around 20% in many cases) or short payment breaks without going back to creditors every time, making it easier to keep an IVA on track if your income dips.
  • Better signposting and transparency: firms are expected to be clearer when an IVA isn’t the best option, and to explain outcomes if an IVA ends early, including access to free, regulated debt advice.

Typical search questions these changes answer

These updates answer common Google searches like “is my house safe in an IVA 2025?”, “IVA equity 10000 rule” and “IVA 60 vs 72 months”. The short version: your home should be better protected, and the extra 12 months of payments now take the place of risky remortgage clauses in most protocol IVAs.

Put simply, the 2025 Protocol is meant to make IVAs safer, fairer and more predictable — especially for homeowners who were previously under pressure to remortgage on poor terms.

How Home Equity Is Calculated Under the 2025 IVA Rules

To understand whether the new rules help you, you first need to know how advisers typically calculate your share of equity for an IVA. This is often called the “IVA equity 10k rule” in blogs and forums.

Step 1 — Start with a realistic property value

Most protocol guidance uses around 85% of the market value instead of the full sale price. This builds in selling costs and avoids overestimating what your property is really worth in an IVA context.

Step 2 — Deduct secured borrowing

From that 85% figure, your IP deducts:

  • your mortgage balance; and
  • any other secured loans (for example, a second charge or secured consolidation loan).

Step 3 — Work out your beneficial share

If you own the property with someone else, the remaining equity is split between you according to the ownership share in your proposal (often 50/50 where there are two joint owners, but this can vary).

Step 4 — Compare your share to the £10,000 threshold

  • If your share of equity is under £10,000, the IVA Protocol 2025 treats it as if you have no usable equity. Your home is excluded and a 5-year IVA will usually be proposed.
  • If your share of equity is £10,000 or more, you are still not forced to remortgage in a protocol IVA. Instead, the term is normally set at 6 years (72 months). Your home is still excluded from realisations unless you voluntarily sell during the IVA term.

Link with mortgage arrears and other priority bills

Remember, an IVA does not write off your mortgage or rent. If you already have mortgage arrears or council tax arrears 2025, you’ll need to look at those alongside any new IVA rules for unsecured debts to see the full picture.

This is a big shift from older protocols, where anyone with modest equity could be pushed towards remortgaging or secured borrowing in the final year, even when that was unrealistic or risky.

Examples: How the New IVA Equity Rules Work in Practice

Example 1 — Equity under £10,000 (5-year IVA)

Sarah & Jamie own a house together:

  • Estimated market value: £200,000
  • 85% IVA valuation: £170,000
  • Mortgage outstanding: £165,000
  • Equity: £5,000 (£170,000 – £165,000)
  • Their share each (50/50): £2,500

Under the IVA Protocol 2025, both of their individual equity shares are under £10,000. That means:

  • Their home is treated as out of the IVA.
  • No one is expected to remortgage or take a secured loan.
  • If an IVA is otherwise suitable, it would generally be proposed for 60 months.

Example 2 — Equity over £10,000 (6-year IVA, no remortgage)

Amir owns his home alone:

  • Estimated market value: £230,000
  • 85% IVA valuation: £195,500
  • Mortgage outstanding: £170,000
  • Equity: £25,500 (£195,500 – £170,000)
  • Amir’s share: £25,500 (sole owner)

Amir’s share of equity is well over £10,000. Under older IVA rules, he might have been expected to try to remortgage in the final year or take out a secured loan — often at poor rates while already in a debt solution.

Under the 2025 Protocol his IP can instead propose:

  • a 72-month (6-year) IVA with no forced remortgage; and
  • his home excluded from IVA realisations (unless he chooses to sell).

The trade-off is simple: an extra year of affordable IVA payments instead of risking his home or taking on expensive secured credit.

Why this matters if you already have high-cost credit

If you’ve previously taken out payday loans or high-cost borrowing to juggle mortgage and card payments, the no-remortgage rule in the new IVA Protocol 2025 can be a big relief. You’re not expected to stack more risky credit on top of existing debt just to satisfy an IVA.

Who Do the IVA Protocol 2025 Changes Apply To — and From When?

The detail is a bit technical, but the timing matters for searchers asking: “What happens if I entered my IVA before 1 July 2025?”

Key dates

  • 1 April 2025: The IVA Protocol 2025 is published with annexes.
  • Until 30 June 2025: providers can still choose to use either the 2021 or 2025 Protocol when setting up a new protocol IVA.
  • From 1 July 2025: all new protocol IVAs are expected to follow the IVA Protocol 2025.

If your IVA was approved before summer 2025

If your IVA was accepted under the old protocol or bespoke terms, it generally stays on those terms. The new Protocol does not automatically rewrite an existing agreement.

However:

  • Your supervisor can still look at current guidance when considering variations (changes to payments or term).
  • If the original agreement has become clearly unaffordable or unsafe, you can ask your supervisor whether a variation or other solution is appropriate — especially if you are a homeowner facing equity issues.

If you’re only now considering an IVA

If your IVA is proposed and agreed from summer 2025 onwards as a protocol IVA, it should be based on the 2025 rules, including:

  • the £10,000 equity threshold for your share of home equity; and
  • no requirement to remortgage your family home in a protocol IVA just to contribute equity.

England & Wales vs Scotland and Northern Ireland

The IVA Protocol 2025 applies to consumer IVAs in England and Wales. If you live in Scotland, look at alternatives such as Trust Deeds and the Debt Arrangement Scheme instead of IVAs. For Northern Ireland, you’ll need Northern Ireland–specific advice, even if search engines still show you “IVA Protocol 2025” results.

Other IVA Protocol 2025 Improvements (Not Just About Your House)

While the media and many blogs focus on home equity, the 2025 Protocol also tightens standards in other areas:

  • Better pre-advice checks: your IP should be clearer that an IVA is one option among others, and signpost you to different solutions if your debt level or circumstances suggest a DMP, DRO or even bankruptcy might be more appropriate.
  • More flexible payment changes: supervisors can usually authorise bigger reductions (often up to around 20%) or payment holidays without calling a full creditor meeting, so IVAs are less likely to fail just because your income dips slightly.
  • Stronger protection for vulnerable clients: there is more focus on vulnerability, communication methods and making sure people understand the long-term implications of an IVA before signing.
  • Clearer completion or early-end rules: if an IVA is completed or terminated early, the new guidance aims to make the process and paperwork (including completion certificates) more consistent.

Does the New IVA Protocol 2025 Make an IVA “Best” for Everyone?

No — and any firm that says otherwise is a red flag.

The 2025 changes make protocol IVAs safer and clearer, especially for homeowners with modest equity, but they don’t magically turn an IVA into the right answer for every debt problem.

IVA might be worth exploring if:

  • You have significant unsecured debt, usually from several creditors.
  • You can afford a consistent monthly payment after essentials are covered.
  • You want legal protection from most creditor action and a clear route to potential write-off at completion.
  • You are a homeowner who wants your home protected but is happy to commit to 5–6 years of structured payments.

You may want to compare with a DMP or DRO if:

  • Your debt level is more modest and could be cleared by reduced interest/frozen interest in a DMP.
  • Your income and assets are low enough that a DRO might be an option.
  • You’re uncomfortable with the formality and public record aspect of an IVA.
  • You want maximum flexibility to adjust payments frequently (often easier in a DMP).

IVA Protocol 2025 — Frequently Asked Questions

Is my home still at risk under the new IVA rules?

In a protocol IVA using the 2025 rules, your family home is generally treated as excluded from realisations. That means the proposal should not require you to remortgage or sell your home just to pay creditors.

However, any secured debts (like your mortgage) still need to be paid as normal, and very high equity compared to your debts can still affect whether an IP feels an IVA is appropriate in the first place.

What happens if I entered my IVA before 1 July 2025?

Your IVA will usually continue under the terms you originally signed. The 2025 Protocol doesn’t automatically rewrite older agreements. If your IVA is proving unaffordable, you can ask your supervisor to look at variations or other options in light of the newer guidance, but they are not obliged to convert an old-style IVA into a new one.

Does the IVA Protocol 2025 apply to every IVA?

No. It applies to protocol IVAs — i.e. those where the provider and creditors have signed up to use the standard protocol documents. Some cases use bespoke terms because the situation is more complex (for example, certain business debts or very high assets).

Always ask your adviser directly: “Will my IVA follow the IVA Protocol 2025, and if not, why not?”

Does the new Protocol change how long an IVA lasts?

The standard expectation is still around 5 years. Under the 2025 rules, a 6-year term is more likely where a homeowner has equity of £10,000 or more, replacing the old expectation that you would try to remortgage near the end.

Will the new IVA rules wipe my mortgage or secured loans?

No. Secured debts such as your mortgage or a secured car loan are treated differently in all IVA protocols. They must continue to be paid if you want to keep the asset. The new rules are about protecting your home from being used as an IVA asset, not writing off your mortgage.

Can I switch from a DMP to an IVA now the rules have changed?

Potentially, yes, but it needs careful advice. Some people start in a Debt Management Plan and later move to an IVA if their situation worsens or they need a more formal outcome. Others do the opposite if their IVA becomes unsustainable.

If you are already in a DMP, it’s sensible to review your full picture — debts, income, equity, and goals — before deciding whether the new Protocol makes an IVA more attractive.

Do IVAs under the 2025 Protocol still show on my credit file?

Yes. Whether it follows the IVA Protocol 2025 or older rules, an IVA normally shows on your credit file for six years from the start date. Even with the improved home protections, you should only choose an IVA if you understand the impact on future borrowing and are comfortable with that trade-off.

Can I get a mortgage after an IVA under the new rules?

Over time, it is often still possible to get a mortgage after an IVA, although many lenders will want to see that the IVA is completed and that you’ve rebuilt your credit. The no-remortgage rule in the 2025 Protocol is about not forcing extra borrowing during your IVA, not about blocking all future mortgage options. For now, the priority is stabilising your debts and protecting your current home.
