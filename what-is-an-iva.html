Fast Definition: What Is an IVA in One Minute?

What is an IVA — the essence

What is an IVA? It’s a formal UK agreement—proposed by a licensed Insolvency Practitioner—where you repay what you can afford for usually 5–6 years. When approved by sufficient creditors, what an IVA is becomes legally binding: interest and charges are typically frozen and remaining eligible unsecured debt can be written off at completion.

What is an IVA outcome?

  • Legal protection for included debts is the core of what an IVA is.
  • One affordable monthly payment defines what an IVA feels like day-to-day.
  • A realistic finish line explains what an IVA offers emotionally—clarity and control.

How an IVA Works: Turning “What Is an IVA” Into Clear Steps

  1. Fact-find — list income, essential costs and every debt. This is the backbone of what an IVA you can maintain.
  2. Proposal — your IP drafts terms explaining what an IVA is and why your offer is fair.
  3. Creditor vote — if 75% (by value) approve, that’s what an IVA is legally: binding on included creditors.

Featured Answer: What Is an IVA in Plain English?

What is an IVA? (30-second answer)

What is an IVA? An IVA (Individual Voluntary Arrangement) is a formal UK agreement where you make one affordable monthly payment—usually for 5–6 years—while most interest and charges on included debts are frozen. If creditors representing 75% (by value) approve your proposal, it becomes legally binding. On successful completion, remaining eligible unsecured debt can be written off. That is what an IVA delivers: protection, affordability, and a finish line.

Why this matters when you search “what is an IVA”

  • Stops most enforcement for included debts once approved
  • Freezes most interest/charges on included debts
  • Creates a realistic, single payment you can defend
  • Provides a clear end point with potential write-off
