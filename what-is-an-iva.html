What Is an IVA? The Calm 2025 UK Guide (No Jargon, Just Answers)

If you’re searching what is an IVA, you likely want a plain-English explanation of how a UK Individual Voluntary Arrangement works, what it does, and whether it fits your real budget. At its heart, what is an IVA about? It’s a legally binding plan between you and your creditors, usually lasting five or six years, where you make one affordable payment while interest and charges on included debts are frozen.

When people ask what is an IVA good for, the answer is stability, space to breathe, and a defined finish line. For a quick snapshot, jump to our main IVA page: iva.html, then come back for deeper detail on what is an IVA in everyday life.

Fast Definition: What Is an IVA in One Minute?

What is an IVA in simple terms? A proposal drafted by a licensed Insolvency Practitioner (IP). If creditors representing at least 75% by debt value vote yes, the IVA becomes legally binding on all—and that’s what an IVA is from day one: protection, affordability, and a path to completion where remaining eligible unsecured debt may be written off.

How It Works: Turning “What Is an IVA” into Clear Steps

  1. Fact-find: List income, essential costs and every debt—this is the backbone of what is an IVA you can keep.
  2. Proposal: The IP drafts terms creditors can accept—explaining what an IVA is and why your offer is fair.
  3. Vote: If creditors (75%+ by value) agree, approval locks in the plan—this is what an IVA means legally.
  4. Protection: Most interest and charges are frozen; creditors must follow the IVA—key to what an IVA is in practice.
  5. Completion: After the term, remaining eligible unsecured balances may be written off.
