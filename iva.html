What Is an IVA (Individual Voluntary Arrangement)?

An IVA is a legally binding agreement with your creditors to repay what you can afford over a fixed period (usually 5‚Äì6 years). Once approved, interest and most enforcement are typically frozen. After successful completion, remaining qualifying unsecured debt is legally written off. It‚Äôs overseen by a licensed Insolvency Practitioner.

What Debts Can Go in an IVA?

Usually included (unsecured): credit/store cards, catalogues, loans, payday loans, overdrafts, BNPL, utility arrears and some HMRC debts.

Not included: secured debts (e.g., mortgages, HP), student loans, child maintenance and most fines. You must maintain secured payments alongside your IVA.
