What Is an IVA?

An IVA (Individual Voluntary Arrangement) is a formal debt solution available in the UK that helps you reduce monthly repayments and write off unaffordable debt. It's designed for people struggling with multiple creditors and debts over £6,000.

Set up by an insolvency practitioner, an IVA is a legally binding agreement between you and your creditors. It protects you from legal action, freezes interest and charges, and stops contact from debt collectors or bailiffs.

Who Qualifies for an IVA?

An IVA isn’t for everyone — but if you meet certain criteria, it could be the best path toward becoming debt-free. You might qualify if:

  • You owe more than £6,000 in unsecured debt
  • You have two or more creditors (e.g., credit cards, loans, HMRC)
  • You live in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland
  • You have a regular income from work, self-employment, or benefits

What Debts Can You Include in an IVA?

One of the best parts of an IVA is that it can include most types of unsecured debt. Here are common debts that can be added:

  • Credit Card Debt
  • Personal Loans
  • Bank Overdrafts
  • Council Tax Arrears
  • Utility Bills (Gas, Electric, Water)
  • Payday Loans
  • Catalogue or Store Cards
  • HMRC Tax Debts
  • Rent Arrears (private or council)

If you’re dealing with bailiffs, enforcement, or constant phone calls — an IVA may be able to stop them.

IVA vs Other Debt Solutions

Unsure whether an IVA is right for you? Here's how it compares to other common UK debt options:

  • Debt Management Plan (DMP) – Flexible but informal. Creditors can still contact you and interest may continue. IVAs legally stop contact and freeze interest.
  • Debt Relief Order (DRO) – Great for people with low income and no assets, but strict eligibility. IVAs suit people with a steady income and higher debt.
  • Bankruptcy – Serious consequences. Public record, may lose home or car. An IVA avoids court and offers more control.
