What Is an IVA?

An Individual Voluntary Arrangement is a formal agreement between you and your creditors to repay debts you can reasonably afford over a set period (usually 5–6 years). It’s managed by a licensed Insolvency Practitioner (IP). Once approved, interest/charges are typically frozen and creditors are generally prevented from taking further enforcement.

How Does an IVA Work?

  • 🧮 Affordable payment: One monthly amount based on a realistic household budget.
  • 🛑 Freeze interest & action: Creditors usually stop interest, charges and most enforcement once approved.
  • 🧭 Managed by an IP: Your licensed Insolvency Practitioner handles proposals, votes and supervision.
  • ✂️ Debt write-off: Any qualifying balance left at the end is written off.

Who Is Eligible for an IVA?

IVAs are typically suitable if you:

  • ✔ Have unsecured debts of around £6,000+ with multiple creditors
  • ✔ Have a reliable income (wages or benefits) to make an affordable contribution
  • ✔ Want to avoid bankruptcy and protect essential assets where possible

Eligibility depends on debts, income, expenses and assets. We’ll run a free check and show the pros and cons, including alternatives if an IVA isn’t the best fit.

  • ✂️ Potential write-off of remaining qualifying unsecured debt at completion

IVA vs Bankruptcy — Which Is Right for You?

Bankruptcy can be quicker for a clean break, but may risk assets and impose stricter restrictions. An IVA offers:

  • More control over assets (home considerations depend on equity)
  • A structured, affordable repayment plan
  • Legal protections without full bankruptcy restrictions

We’ll compare both routes privately and recommend the safest option for your goals.

The IVA Process (Step-by-Step)

  1. Free assessment: We review debts, income, spending and assets.
  2. Budget & proposal: Your IP drafts an affordable plan.
  3. Creditor vote: 75% by value must agree (modifications possible).
  4. Approval: Protections usually apply; interest/enforcement typically paused.
  5. Ongoing support: Supervision and annual reviews keep payments realistic.
  6. Completion: Remaining qualifying unsecured debts are written off.

Which Debts Can You Include in an IVA?

Usually included:

  • Credit cards, store cards and catalogues
  • Personal loans and overdrafts
  • Utility arrears and some HMRC debts
  • Buy-Now-Pay-Later and similar unsecured credit

Not included: Secured debts (e.g., mortgages, HP car finance). These run alongside your IVA, and you must maintain payments. Certain debts like child maintenance and some fines are not usually written off.

How an IVA Stops Bailiffs & Legal Action

Once your IVA is approved, creditors generally must stop or not start most enforcement (like bailiff visits or new court claims), provided you comply with the IVA terms. This protection helps you focus on affordable repayments.

  • 🚪 Bailiffs typically cannot enforce once the IVA is in place
  • 📨 Letters and calls should reduce significantly
  • ⚖️ New legal action is usually restricted

IVA and Your Credit File

An IVA will appear on your credit report for six years from the start date. During this period you may find it harder or more expensive to obtain credit. Many people find that completing an IVA gives a clearer path to rebuilding credit than ongoing defaults or informal arrangements that drag on.

Can You Keep Your Home During an IVA?

Often yes. Your IP will assess equity and affordability. In some cases, equity release or a modest extension to the IVA may be proposed instead of selling. Keeping mortgage payments up to date is essential.

  • 🏠 Equity may affect terms (not an automatic sale)
  • 💬 We’ll explain options like remortgage or 12-month extensions

Understanding IVA Costs & Fees

IVA fees are set out clearly in your proposal, agreed with creditors and taken from your monthly contributions — not added on top.

  • 🧾 Set-up (Nominee) fee
  • 🔄 Supervision (ongoing) fee
  • ✅ Completion fee

We’ll explain all costs before you decide, and show how they fit within your affordable payment.

Alternatives to an IVA

If an IVA isn’t suitable, we’ll recommend safer alternatives:

  • Debt Management Plan (DMP) — informal, flexible payments (interest freeze not guaranteed)
  • Debt Relief Order (DRO) — for low income/low assets within set limits
  • Bankruptcy — formal insolvency with faster discharge but stricter impacts
  • Breathing Space — 60-day pause while you choose a solution

IVA — Frequently Asked Questions

How long does an IVA last?

Most IVAs last 5–6 years. In some cases an extra 12 months may be proposed if equity can’t be released.

Will my creditors keep contacting me?

Contact should reduce significantly after approval. If you receive calls or letters, let us know — we’ll help ensure protections are respected.

Can I change my payment if my income changes?

Yes, your IP can review the budget. Payments may be adjusted if your circumstances change.

Are you regulated and who oversees IVAs?

Debt advice is regulated by the FCA. IVAs are administered by licensed Insolvency Practitioners and overseen by the Insolvency Service. For impartial advice you can also speak to StepChange or National Debtline.

How do you get paid? Do you earn commission?

We explain any fees before you decide. Where a third party provides a formal solution, their fees are disclosed in writing in your proposal.
