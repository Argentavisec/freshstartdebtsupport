What Is an IVA?

An IVA (Individual Voluntary Arrangement) is a legally binding debt solution that allows you to repay a portion of your debts over a set period, usually five years. At the end of the IVA term, any remaining unsecured debts are typically written off. It’s a popular alternative to bankruptcy and is often searched on Google alongside terms like “write off debt” and “breathing space”.

The interest in IVAs remains strong in the UK, as shown by rising Google Trends data. Thousands of people each month search for solutions such as “IVA help” and “best IVA company”. If your debts feel unmanageable, this could be your route to financial freedom.

How an IVA Works

An IVA is arranged through a licensed insolvency practitioner. They help you create an affordable monthly repayment plan based on your income and essential living costs. You’ll only pay what you can realistically afford — and after the IVA period ends, the rest of your debt is usually wiped.

Searches for "individual voluntary arrangement UK" and "how does an IVA work" have increased steadily, indicating that more people are seeking structured ways out of debt. IVAs also provide breathing space from bailiffs, as once your IVA is approved, creditors must stop all enforcement action.

Who Qualifies for an IVA?

To be eligible for an IVA, you generally need to:

  • Owe over £6,000 to two or more creditors
  • Live in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland
  • Have a regular income
  • Struggle to repay debts in full

IVA vs Debt Management Plan (DMP)

Many people compare an IVA with a Debt Management Plan (DMP). A DMP is an informal arrangement where you repay all of your debt, but interest isn’t always frozen. An IVA, on the other hand, is legally binding and offers a chance to write off unaffordable debt.

Searches like “IVA or DMP” and “which is better IVA or DMP” have spiked in recent months, showing more people are weighing their options. If your debt is large and you want a clear end date, an IVA may be more effective.
