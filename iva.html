What is an IVA? Understanding Individual Voluntary Arrangements

An Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA) is a legally binding agreement between you and your creditors to repay your debts over an agreed period, usually five or six years. It is a formal alternative to bankruptcy and can help you write off a significant portion of your unsecured debts.

At Fresh Start Debt Support, we specialise in guiding people through the IVA process, ensuring you understand your options and helping you achieve a fresh financial start.

IVA or Bankruptcy: Which Debt Solution is Right for You?

While bankruptcy offers a quick way to write off debts, it comes with more severe consequences like loss of assets and longer restrictions. An IVA, on the other hand, provides:

  • More control over your assets, including your home
  • A fixed repayment plan with the potential to write off some debts
  • A formal arrangement that protects you from creditors without total financial restrictions

Bankruptcy may be better suited for those with minimal assets or no affordable repayment options. We‚Äôll help you evaluate which solution aligns with your goals and circumstances.

  2. Proposal Drafting: Our insolvency practitioner drafts a repayment proposal based on what you can afford.

Which Debts Can You Include in an IVA?

An IVA typically covers most unsecured debts, such as:

  • Credit cards
  • Personal loans
  • Store and catalogue debts
  • Overdrafts
  • Utility arrears
  • Tax debts and HMRC arrears

Secured debts, like mortgages or car loans, are usually excluded but can be managed alongside an IVA.

How IVAs Stop Bailiffs and Legal Action

Once your IVA is approved, your creditors must stop all legal actions, including:

  • Visiting your home or seizing possessions
  • Sending court summons or enforcement notices
  • Making phone calls or sending letters demanding payment

This legal protection reduces stress and lets you focus on repaying debts affordably.

How an IVA Affects Your Credit Rating

An IVA will appear on your credit file for six years from the start date, which can make obtaining credit more difficult during that time.

However, an IVA is often better for rebuilding credit compared to bankruptcy because it shows you are actively managing your debts responsibly.

Keeping Your Home While on an IVA

One of the biggest concerns is whether you can keep your home while in an IVA. Generally:

  • If you have equity in your home, you may need to release some value as part of your IVA payments
  • If your mortgage is affordable and you keep up with payments, your home is usually safe
  • We advise on options like equity release or selling the property if necessary

Understanding IVA Costs and Fees

IVAs involve fees charged by the insolvency practitioner, which are agreed with your creditors and included in your monthly payments.

Typical fees include:

  • Set-up fee for preparing the IVA proposal
  • Monthly supervision fees throughout the IVA term
  • Completion fee at the end of the IVA

We explain all costs upfront and help you understand how these fees impact your monthly payments.
