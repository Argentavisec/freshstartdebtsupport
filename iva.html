Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA)

An Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA) is a formal solution that allows you to reduce your unsecured debts by making affordable payments over a fixed period — usually five years. At the end of the term, any remaining qualifying debt is legally written off.

How Does an IVA Work?

Once approved, an IVA legally protects you from creditor action. Your debts are frozen, interest and charges stop, and you make just one affordable monthly payment based on your income and expenses. This payment is divided fairly between your creditors.

Who Qualifies for an IVA?

IVAs are ideal for people with:

  • Over £6,000 in unsecured debt
  • Two or more creditors
  • A regular income (employed or self-employed)
  • Struggling with debt repayments

What Debts Can Be Included?

Most unsecured debts can be included in an IVA, such as:

  • Credit cards and store cards
  • Personal loans
  • Overdrafts
  • Payday loans
  • Catalogue debts
  • Council tax arrears
  • Utility arrears
  • Tax or HMRC debts

Secured debts like mortgages or car finance usually cannot be included. But your specialist will help you prioritise these separately.

How Is an IVA Different From a DMP?

Unlike a Debt Management Plan (DMP), an IVA is a legally binding agreement and includes debt write-off. A DMP is informal and usually doesn’t involve any debt being written off. Depending on your situation, one may be more suitable than the other.
