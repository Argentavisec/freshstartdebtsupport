Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA)

An IVA is a formal debt solution approved by your creditors, allowing you to make one affordable monthly payment. After five or six years, any remaining qualifying debt is legally written off. IVAs protect you from legal action and bailiffs, and they help rebuild your financial future.

Debt Management Plan (DMP)

A DMP is an informal agreement with creditors to pay back your debts at an affordable rate. It can freeze interest and charges, giving you breathing room without the need for legal formalities. Ideal for those with a steady income but who want to avoid formal insolvency.

Debt Relief Order (DRO)

For people with low income and minimal assets, a DRO offers a way to freeze debts for 12 months. If financial circumstances don‚Äôt improve, the debts are legally written off, providing a fresh start without ongoing payments.

Bankruptcy & Trust Deeds

When other options are exhausted, bankruptcy or Scottish Trust Deeds may offer relief. These solutions carry long-term impacts, so it‚Äôs vital to understand your options thoroughly. Visit our comparison page for details.

Debt Write-Off: What Does It Mean and How Can You Qualify?

Debt write-off means legally removing some or all of your debt so you no longer owe it. This is usually part of formal debt solutions and depends on your financial situation and the type of debt.

Common ways debt write-off happens include:

  • IVAs: After 5-6 years of payments, remaining unsecured debts can be written off.
  • Debt Relief Orders: Debts frozen for 12 months and written off if financial situation stays the same.
  • Bankruptcy: After bankruptcy, most debts are discharged following a period of typically one year.

Not everyone qualifies for debt write-off, and it‚Äôs important to seek expert advice to understand your eligibility.

When you have too much debt, debt consolidation can be an option worth considering. It involves combining multiple debts into a single loan or repayment plan, potentially reducing your monthly outgoings.
