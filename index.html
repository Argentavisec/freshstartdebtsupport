Which Debt Help Route Fits Me?

Debt Management Plan (DMP)

Flexible and informal. Many creditors freeze or reduce interest and charges. Great if you want adaptability and can maintain a steady payment but prefer to avoid formal insolvency.

Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA)

Formal, legally binding. One affordable monthly payment; on successful completion, remaining eligible unsecured debt may be written off. Provides strong protection from most creditor action.

Debt Relief Order (DRO)

For low income/low assets. Debts are usually frozen for 12 months and written off if your situation doesn‚Äôt improve. Strict eligibility applies.

Breathing Space UK ‚Äî How to Get the 60-Day Legal Pause

Breathing Space (Debt Respite Scheme) is a government legal protection that can pause most interest, fees and creditor enforcement for up to 60 days while you get debt advice and set up a solution. It‚Äôs administered by the Insolvency Service and typically requires a debt adviser to start it for you.

What it usually covers

  • Most unsecured debts (credit cards, loans, overdrafts, BNPL, arrears)
  • Pauses most enforcement and new interest/charges during the period

What it doesn‚Äôt cover

  • Ongoing priority bills still need paying where possible (rent, council tax, utilities)
  • Some debts are excluded or treated differently (e.g., criminal fines)

Official guidance: GOV.UK ‚Äî options for dealing with your debts and the Debt Respite Scheme Regulations 2020. Independent explainers: StepChange.

CCJ Help ‚Äî How to Stop, Set Aside, or Remove a County Court Judgment

A CCJ happens after a claim is issued and either admitted or not defended. If you receive a claim form, you generally have 14 days to respond (or acknowledge to get 28). Paying a CCJ in full within one month usually removes it from the public register; after that, it‚Äôs marked as ‚Äúsatisfied‚Äù if paid.

  • Before judgment: Defend, admit with an offer, or settle.
  • After judgment: Apply to vary if the payment is unaffordable, or to set aside if there‚Äôs a valid reason (e.g., papers never received).
  • Pay within a month to remove it; otherwise it stays for 6 years.

Official guidance: GOV.UK ‚Äî CCJs overview and GOV.UK ‚Äî CCJ for debt.

Most unsecured debts in England & Wales become statute-barred if there‚Äôs been no court claim, no written acknowledgment and no payment for 6 years (different rules for mortgage shortfalls and some debts). A CCJ never becomes statute-barred ‚Äî it must be resolved.

  • If you think a debt‚Äôs too old to enforce, don‚Äôt pay or admit it before checking.
  • Ask the creditor for proof of liability and last payment/activity date.
