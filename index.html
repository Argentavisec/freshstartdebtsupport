Which Debt Help Route Fits Me?

Debt Management Plan (DMP)

Flexible and informal. Many creditors freeze or reduce interest and charges. Great if you want adaptability and can maintain a steady payment but prefer to avoid formal insolvency.

Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA)

Formal, legally binding. One affordable monthly payment; on successful completion, remaining eligible unsecured debt may be written off. Provides strong protection from most creditor action.

Debt Relief Order (DRO)

For low income/low assets. Debts are usually frozen for 12 months and written off if your situation doesn’t improve. Strict eligibility applies.

Debt Help Frequently Asked Questions

How fast can you start helping once I complete the JotForm?

We’ll try to call you promptly (and WhatsApp if we can’t get through). From there, we start your fact-find and budgeting immediately, prioritising urgent risks like council tax debt and utility bills.

Do I need to list every debt before we speak?

No. If you’re unsure, we’ll guide a free credit check to identify missing accounts. We’ll also ask questions to pick up priority debts you might not have to hand.

Will a DMP or IVA stop calls and letters?

They usually reduce contact significantly once in place. We also help you use Breathing Space appropriately, and we issue a reference number you can quote to creditors while your plan is being set up.

Will my credit score be affected?

Debt solutions can affect your credit file. But for many people, the priority is stabilising the household, protecting essentials and stopping the spiral. We’ll talk through the trade-offs clearly.

Can debts be written off?

Some formal routes allow for write-off at completion (e.g., a successful IVA) or after a qualifying period (e.g., an eligible DRO). We’ll confirm eligibility and implications before you choose.

What if my income changes later?

We’ll help you set a plan with realistic headroom. If your income changes, we can review and adjust where the solution allows (for example, DMPs are typically more flexible; IVAs have formal variation processes).

I’m self-employed. Can you still help?

Yes. We regularly support sole traders and company directors. See Business Debt and we’ll tailor your route to variable income.

Breathing Space UK — How to Get the 60-Day Legal Pause

Breathing Space (Debt Respite Scheme) is a government legal protection that can pause most interest, fees and creditor enforcement for up to 60 days while you get debt advice and set up a solution. It’s administered by the Insolvency Service and typically requires a debt adviser to start it for you.

What it usually covers

  • Most unsecured debts (credit cards, loans, overdrafts, BNPL, arrears)
  • Pauses most enforcement and new interest/charges during the period

What it doesn’t cover

  • Ongoing priority bills still need paying where possible (rent, council tax, utilities)
  • Some debts are excluded or treated differently (e.g., criminal fines)

Official guidance: GOV.UK — options for dealing with your debts and the Debt Respite Scheme Regulations 2020. Independent explainers: StepChange.

Stop Council Tax Bailiffs (Enforcement Agents) — What to Do Today

If you’ve had a council tax bailiff/enforcement letter or visit, act quickly. In England & Wales, bailiffs can’t force entry for council tax on a first visit. You can usually arrange affordable payments and ask them not to visit if a plan is in place, especially if you’re vulnerable.

Immediate steps

  • Don’t let them in. Speak through the door and arrange payments in writing/phone.
  • Prioritise essentials, then make a realistic offer — not a one-off that fails next month.
  • Tell us if you’re vulnerable (health, disability, children); extra protections may apply.

Read more at Citizens Advice — if a bailiff visits you and their council tax bailiffs guide.

CCJ Help — How to Stop, Set Aside, or Remove a County Court Judgment

A CCJ happens after a claim is issued and either admitted or not defended. If you receive a claim form, you generally have 14 days to respond (or acknowledge to get 28). Paying a CCJ in full within one month usually removes it from the public register; after that, it’s marked as “satisfied” if paid.

Your options

  • Before judgment: Defend, admit with an offer, or settle.
  • After judgment: Apply to vary if the payment is unaffordable, or to set aside if there’s a valid reason (e.g., papers never received).
  • Pay within a month to remove it; otherwise it stays for 6 years.

Official guidance: GOV.UK — CCJs overview and GOV.UK — CCJ for debt.

Attachment of Earnings Order — Stop or Reduce Deductions from Wages

An Attachment of Earnings Order (AEO) tells your employer to take money from your wages to pay a judgment debt. If it’s unaffordable, you can usually apply to vary or suspend the order by showing your budget and circumstances.

What to do

  • Prepare a realistic income & expenditure (we can help) and evidence.
  • Apply to vary/suspend using the court’s process (ask the court named on your order).
  • Consider a formal solution (DMP/IVA/DRO) to replace chaotic deductions with one plan.

See the rules in the Civil Procedure Rules — Part 89 (Attachment of Earnings) and practical guidance via Citizens Advice.

Statute-Barred Debt — Does the 6-Year Rule Apply to You?

Most unsecured debts in England & Wales become statute-barred if there’s been no court claim, no written acknowledgment and no payment for 6 years (different rules for mortgage shortfalls and some debts). A CCJ never becomes statute-barred — it must be resolved.

Key points

  • If you think a debt’s too old to enforce, don’t pay or admit it before checking.
  • Ask the creditor for proof of liability and last payment/activity date.
  • Some debts have different limitation periods.

Learn more from Citizens Advice — statute-barred debts.

If You’re Being Chased for an Old Debt

Buy Now Pay Later (Klarna, Clearpay) — What If You Can’t Pay?

BNPL is easy to stack across different merchants. If you fall behind, treat it like any other unsecured credit: list every agreement, prioritise essentials, and offer a realistic plan. Consider consolidating into a single managed solution if the total is unaffordable.

Practical steps

  • Stop new BNPL spending and cancel non-essentials.
  • Tell the provider you’re seeking debt help and request breathing room on interest/charges.
  • Add BNPL to your DMP/IVA assessment so nothing is missed.

Independent guidance: MoneyHelper — Buy Now Pay Later.

£1 Token Payment Plan — When It’s Sensible and How to Ask

When your budget is under severe pressure, a short-term token payment (e.g., £1 per creditor) can hold the line while you stabilise essentials or wait for a longer-term solution. Explain your circumstances, provide your budget, and ask for a freeze on interest/charges if possible.

Good uses

  • While waiting for benefits, a new job, or a formal debt solution to start
  • During Breathing Space or a temporary crisis
