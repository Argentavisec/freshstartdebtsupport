RBS Customers: Practical Help to Stop Things Snowballing

If you bank with Royal Bank of Scotland (RBS) and are falling behind on a credit card, overdraft or loan, there are two tracks to help you quickly: (1) speak to RBS’s financial difficulty team to reduce pressure on your bank products, and (2) use Breathing Space plus a realistic budget to set up a plan across all debts — often a DMP, IVA or DRO. We’ll guide you through both.

Quick script to contact RBS:
“I’m in financial difficulty and need help with my [credit card/overdraft/loan]. My income has changed and I’m getting advice. Can you review interest/charges and set a temporary, affordable payment while I sort my plan?”

What Help Can RBS Offer If You’re Struggling?

  • Financial difficulty teams: explain your situation and request temporary help (reduced payments, interest/fee relief where appropriate).
  • Overdraft support: ask for limit review and fee/interest relief while your plan is set up.
  • Loan & credit card help: ask about affordable payment arrangements and interest options based on your verified budget.
  • Accessibility & vulnerability support: reasonable adjustments if you have additional needs.

We’ll help you prepare a defendable budget (verified with bank statements) so RBS and other creditors see fair, consistent figures.

Breathing Space — Pause Interest, Charges & Most Enforcement

In England & Wales, Breathing Space can pause interest, charges and most enforcement on qualifying debts while you set up a plan with an adviser. We’ll check eligibility and help you use this protection properly.

  • Standard Breathing Space: up to 60 days of protection
  • Mental Health route: during crisis treatment + 30 days

Breathing Space isn’t a payment holiday — you should still make affordable payments where possible. It’s a window to set up the right plan with fewer threats and fees.

Answering High-Intent RBS Searches

“RBS debt help” — who do I speak to and what do I say?

Use the script above to reach RBS’s financial difficulty team. We’ll help you prepare a verified budget so affordable temporary payments are more likely to be accepted.

“RBS overdraft help / charges mounting” — what’s realistic?

Ask for limit/fee/interest review while you set up a formal plan. We ensure your essential bills are protected first, then propose a fair payment to all creditors.

“Can RBS freeze interest if I enter a DMP?”

Interest/charges are at creditors’ discretion. Many freeze or reduce them when your budget is realistic and consistently paid. A DMP can be flexible and informal.

“IVA with RBS debts — will they agree?”

Approval depends on your creditors and affordability. If approved and completed, an IVA can write off remaining qualifying unsecured debt.

“DRO fee scrapped — do I qualify?”

The £90 fee was removed in April 2024. A DRO suits low income/asset cases within the debt limits and criteria — we’ll check quickly.

RBS Debt Help — FAQs

Will contacting RBS hurt my credit score?

Talking to RBS doesn’t itself hurt your score. Missed payments, defaults and arrangements may show. A fair, verified plan usually beats letting arrears build.

How fast can pressure ease?

With protections like Breathing Space or a formal solution, most new enforcement is paused. We also notify creditors in writing and set clear next steps.

Can I include RBS and non-RBS debts together?

Yes. We build one budget that covers all unsecured debts, not just RBS, so payments are fair and sustainable.

I’m in Scotland — are options different?

Yes, different schemes apply (e.g., moratorium, Trust Deeds, DAS). Tell us your postcode and we’ll signpost correctly.

What documents do you need from me?

Recent bank statements, payslips/benefit letters, creditor statements and balances. We use these to create a defendable budget creditors accept.
