Why Council Tax Arrears Are a Priority (and What That Means)

Unlike many other debts, council tax debt is classed as a priority. Councils can act faster than most creditors. If arrears build up, they can apply for a Liability Order, add fees, and instruct enforcement agents (bailiffs). They may also seek deductions from wages or benefits.

Options That Can Include Council Tax Debt

Most people with council tax debt are also juggling other arrears (credit cards, overdrafts, BNPL, loans). Bringing everything into one plan often works best.

  • IVA (Individual Voluntary Arrangement) ‚Äì one affordable monthly payment, legal protection from creditors, and potential write-off of remaining eligible unsecured debt at the end.
  • DMP (Debt Management Plan) ‚Äì an informal route that can reduce monthly payments; many creditors will freeze or reduce interest/charges.
  • DRO (Debt Relief Order) ‚Äì for low income/low assets; debts are frozen for 12 months and then written off if circumstances do not improve.
  • Bankruptcy ‚Äì sometimes the quickest reset if debt is completely unmanageable.

Dealing with Bailiffs Over Council Tax Debt

Enforcement agents (bailiffs) collect council tax after a Liability Order. You still have rights and options:

  • They cannot force entry for council tax on a first visit‚Äîdo not let them in.
  • You can communicate via letter/phone and arrange an affordable plan.
  • Bailiff fees can be significant; stopping visits quickly saves money.
