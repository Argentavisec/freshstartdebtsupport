Behind on Council Tax? Get Immediate Help Today

Fallen behind on council tax payments? You’re not alone. Councils across the UK act quickly — often sending bailiffs or taking court action within weeks. But the good news is: you can stop enforcement, reduce payments, and protect your household with the right support.

Stop Council Action Now

What Happens If You Don’t Pay Council Tax?

  • Full year's tax becomes immediately due
  • You may receive a Magistrates' Court summons
  • Council can apply for a liability order
  • Bailiffs (enforcement agents) may visit your home
  • Wage or benefit deductions may be enforced

Many councils use private enforcement firms like Marston Holdings, Jacobs Enforcement or Equita. We can help stop them from taking further action.

Can You Stop Bailiffs from Collecting Council Tax?

Yes. Once you begin a formal debt solution such as an IVA or Debt Management Plan, enforcement must pause. In many cases, bailiff action is suspended as soon as you appoint a representative like Fresh Start Debt Support.

Affordable Council Tax Repayment Options

You may qualify for a formal plan that reduces your monthly payments and protects you from further action:

  • IVA (Individual Voluntary Arrangement) – council tax and other debts written off after 5 years
  • DMP (Debt Management Plan) – reduce payments and freeze interest
  • DRO (Debt Relief Order) – for low income and minimal assets

We’ll help you choose the right path based on your income, bills, and situation.

Can I Include Other Debts Too?

Absolutely. Council tax arrears are often just one part of the problem. We can help combine other debts like:

  • Rent arrears
  • Credit card debt
  • Personal loans
  • Gas and electric bills

These can be managed together through a single monthly payment that suits your budget.

Why Act Fast With Council Tax Debt?

Councils can act quicker than most creditors. Waiting too long can lead to bailiff fees, vehicle clamping, or deductions from your wages or Universal Credit. Acting now gives you more control and less stress.

Our team can contact the council on your behalf within hours — offering protection and immediate peace of mind.

Worried About Housing Disrepair Too?

In many cases, those falling behind on council tax also suffer from poor living conditions. If your council home or housing association property has problems like mould, damp, leaks, or unsafe electrics, you could be eligible to make a claim — completely separate from your debt situation.

Disrepair can contribute to financial hardship by damaging your belongings, increasing energy bills, or even affecting your health. If you’ve reported these issues to your local authority and they’ve failed to act within a reasonable timeframe, you may be entitled to compensation and repairs at no cost to you.

What can you claim for?

  • Compensation for stress, inconvenience, or health impacts
  • Repair delays after multiple complaints
  • Damage to clothing, furniture, or property from leaks or mould
  • Heating issues during winter or unsafe conditions

Our partner site, Home Justice Solutions, specialises in helping council and housing association tenants across the UK hold landlords accountable. The process is fast, no-win-no-fee, and doesn’t affect your rent or tenancy status.

Already helped by us with debt? You can make a separate claim for housing disrepair too. We treat all claims confidentially and professionally.
