Options for HSBC Debt: Choose a Route That Fits Your Life

Debt Management Plan (DMP)

Informal, flexible and based on what you can genuinely afford. Many creditors reduce or freeze interest/charges (not guaranteed). Good if you need adaptability and want to avoid formal insolvency.

Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA)

A formal, legally binding agreement. One affordable monthly payment; if you complete successfully, remaining eligible unsecured debt may be written off. Strong protection from most creditor action.

Debt Relief Order (DRO)

Designed for low income/low assets. Debts are normally frozen for 12 months and then written off if your situation hasn’t improved. Strict eligibility applies.

HSBC Debt FAQs

Will HSBC freeze interest and charges if I start a DMP?

Many creditors reduce or freeze interest/charges on DMPs, but it isn’t guaranteed. We submit a budget-based proposal and explain your circumstances to support the request.

Can HSBC pass or sell my account to a debt collector?

Yes, that can happen if arrears persist. If it does, read What is debt collection? and Stop Debt Collectors, then check the relevant agency page (e.g., Lowell, Cabot, Moorcroft, Wescot, PRA Group).

Will a plan hurt my credit file?

Debt solutions can affect your credit file. For many people, stabilising the household, protecting essentials and stopping the spiral is the priority. We’ll explain trade-offs clearly before you decide.

I can’t afford anything right now—what do I do?

We’ll check whether Breathing Space is appropriate, then build a realistic plan with headroom. If you meet criteria, a DRO may also be an option.

Is an IVA better than a DMP for HSBC debt?

It depends on your income, assets and goals. An IVA can write off remaining eligible unsecured debt on successful completion, but it’s formal and structured. A DMP is more flexible. See Compare Solutions or ask for tailored guidance.

Can I include my HSBC overdraft?

Yes. Overdrafts, cards and loans can all be included in a single plan. Breaking the overdraft cycle is often a turning point for clients.

HSBC Persistent Debt Letter — What It Means & Next Steps

“Persistent debt” usually means that over the last 18 months you’ve paid more in interest/fees than you’ve repaid of the amount borrowed on your HSBC credit card. Don’t panic—shift to a budget-based plan, stop new card use, and consider DMP, IVA or DRO depending on your situation.
