Who Are Hoist Finance & Why Are They Contacting Me?

Hoist Finance are a debt collection company. They either own the debt (it’s been purchased from the original lender) or they’re collecting on behalf of a lender or another owner. Their role is to request payment or agree a plan. You’re allowed to ask questions, request details and make an affordable proposal rather than agreeing on the spot.

Common Debt Types Hoist Finance May Pursue

  • Credit & store cards and catalogues / BNPL
  • Personal loans and overdrafts
  • Utility accounts (energy, water, etc.)
  • Telecoms/subscription balances

Are They Legitimate?

Debt collectors are permitted to contact you, but they must treat you fairly. You don’t have to accept harassment, misleading statements or pressure for unaffordable payments. If anything looks off, we’ll sanity-check the letters and help you respond. See our plain-English guide: Stop Debt Collectors.

Understanding Hoist Finance Letters, Calls & Visits

Contact often ramps up over time. Knowing what each type of contact means can keep things calm and on track.

Letters & Emails

Usually a statement of balance and a request to pay or get in touch. Save everything and note reference numbers. If you’re unsure about the details, ask for information in writing and avoid on-the-spot commitments.

Texts & Phone Calls

It’s okay to answer or to keep communication in writing. If you do pick up, you can say you’re seeking help and will come back with a realistic proposal. Build your budget first with the Debt Calculator.

Doorstep Visits

A debt collector is not a bailiff and cannot force entry. You’re entitled to ask them to leave and to communicate in writing. If visits worry you, read Stop Debt Collectors and consider Breathing Space while we set up your plan.

Tip: Keep It Written Where Possible

Written communication reduces the risk of agreeing to something you can’t afford under pressure. It also creates a clear record of what’s been said.

Turning Hoist Finance Pressure into One Affordable Plan

Most people we support have multiple debts. Where suitable, we roll them into one manageable monthly payment so life gets simpler and contact pressure reduces.

Debt Management Plan (DMP)

An informal route with one affordable payment shared across creditors. Many freeze or reduce interest and charges (not guaranteed). Good flexibility if your income varies.

Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA)

A formal, legally binding solution. You pay what’s affordable for an agreed term; any remaining eligible unsecured debt can be written off on successful completion. Offers strong protection from most creditor action once approved.

Debt Relief Order (DRO)

For low income/low assets within eligibility limits. Debts are usually frozen for 12 months then written off if your circumstances haven’t improved. Strict criteria apply.

Hoist Finance: Frequently Asked Questions

Will Hoist Finance take me to court?

Collectors don’t automatically go to court. If court is considered, you’ll usually get formal pre-action paperwork first. Don’t ignore it—respond on time. We’ll help prioritise a route that protects your essentials while you address the claim.

Can Hoist Finance enter my home?

No. A debt collector is not a bailiff and cannot force entry. If someone visits, you can ask them to leave and communicate in writing. See Stop Debt Collectors.

Should I answer Hoist Finance’s calls?

It’s your choice. If you answer, you can confirm you’re seeking help and avoid agreeing to payments before your budget is set. We can provide a reference number once we’re supporting you.

What if the debt isn’t mine or the amount is wrong?

Tell them you dispute the account and request information. We’ll help verify via a free credit check and guide your response. Don’t pay until it’s clear.

Can I include Hoist Finance in a DMP or IVA?

Yes, if the debt is eligible. In a DMP, you make one affordable payment spread across creditors. In an IVA, the arrangement is legally binding and offers strong protection from most creditor action once approved.

Will a DMP or IVA affect my credit file?

Debt solutions can affect your credit. Many people prioritise stabilising the household and reducing stress. We’ll explain trade-offs clearly so you can choose confidently.

Can debts be written off?

Some formal routes allow write-off at the end—for example, remaining eligible unsecured debts can be written off at successful completion of an IVA. An eligible DRO can write off debts after the moratorium if your situation doesn’t improve. We’ll check eligibility and implications first.

What if I’m also hearing from other collectors?

We can bring multiple debts under one plan where suitable. See our pages for Lowell, Cabot, Moorcroft, Advantis, Link Financial, Capquest, Wescot, Resolvecall, Robinson Way, Fredrickson, Moriarty Law, Drydensfairfax and PRA Group.
