Formal Debt Solutions to Help With Too Many Outgoings

Sometimes, informal budgeting isn’t enough to solve the problem of too many outgoings. That’s where legal debt solutions come in:

Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA)

An IVA is a formal debt solution where you pay what you can afford monthly for up to 6 years. After that, remaining eligible debts are written off. It stops creditor harassment and bailiff action.

Debt Relief Order (DRO)

Designed for low income individuals with few assets, a DRO freezes debts for 12 months and writes them off if your finances don’t improve.

Debt Management Plan (DMP)

A flexible repayment plan where creditors agree to freeze interest and accept reduced payments until debts are cleared. Ideal if you have steady income but need help managing payments.

Each solution is tailored to different circumstances — we’ll help you pick the best fit based on your income, debts, and outgoings.

How an Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA) Can Reduce Your Outgoings

An IVA is a legally binding agreement between you and your creditors to pay back what you can afford over a set period, usually five to six years. It consolidates your debts into a single monthly payment, often significantly lower than your current total outgoings.

IVAs offer protection from creditor harassment and bailiff action, and after completion, any remaining debt is written off. It’s an excellent solution if your outgoings have become unmanageable due to multiple debts.

Our experts will assess your situation and help you understand if an IVA is the right option for you.

Debt Relief Orders (DROs) for Low Income and Minimal Outgoings

If you have very low income and minimal assets, a Debt Relief Order (DRO) can provide legal protection and a route to write off your debts after 12 months. DROs freeze enforcement action, giving you breathing space.

DROs are often suitable for those whose outgoings leave no room to pay unsecured debts. Strict eligibility criteria apply, but our team will guide you through the process and help you apply.

Using Debt Management Plans (DMPs) to Manage Too Many Outgoings

A Debt Management Plan (DMP) is an informal arrangement with your creditors to repay debts over time at reduced amounts. While it doesn’t legally freeze debt or bailiff action, many creditors will pause enforcement during a DMP.

DMPs can help you regain control of your outgoings by spreading payments over a longer period, but they require discipline and communication.
