Who Are Resolvecall and Why Are They Contacting Me?

Resolvecall is a UK debt collection agency that often uses telephone, letters and sometimes doorstep visits to make contact. They might be collecting on behalf of an original lender or a separate debt purchaser, or they could be contacting you because a previous creditor has assigned or sold the account.

Typical accounts include credit cards, personal loans, overdrafts, retail finance and catalogues or BNPL (e.g., Klarna). Even if you’re unsure what the debt is, don’t panic—there’s a safe order of steps that keeps you in control.

Are Resolvecall Allowed to Visit My Home?

Debt collectors are not bailiffs. A collector visiting your home has no right of entry and cannot seize goods. You can ask them to leave. Most matters are handled in writing or by phone. If visits worry you, we can help move contact to writing while we set your plan.

Should I Ignore Resolvecall?

Ignoring contact usually increases stress and can lead to escalation. A steady response—verifying the debt, protecting essentials and putting forward a defendable proposal—works best. We can do this with you step by step.

Your Rights When Resolvecall Gets in Touch

You have the right to fair treatment and clear information. You can ask for the account details, request contact in writing, and take the time you need to build an accurate budget before you commit to payment.

H3: Priority Bills Come First—Always

Never agree to something that risks your household. Stabilise rent/mortgage, utilities and council tax before tackling non-priority debts. This approach keeps you safer and prevents new emergencies.

H3: Ask for the Key Facts

Request the original creditor, current balance, account reference and a breakdown of how the figure was reached. For regulated credit (cards/loans), you can request a copy of the agreement. We’ll help you interpret any documents.

H3: Consider Breathing Space if Pressure Is High

Breathing Space is designed to pause most interest, fees and enforcement for a limited period while we build your long-term route. It can be very effective when multiple creditors are contacting you.

H3: Only Offer What’s Truly Affordable

Good negotiations start with a realistic budget. Test your figures with our Debt Calculator or build your budget with us one-to-one. A defendable offer is more likely to be accepted.

Doorstep Visits from Resolvecall: What to Expect

Some collectors use doorstep agents to “engage” people who haven’t responded by phone or letter. Remember: a debt collector is not an enforcement agent. You don’t have to let them in, and you can ask them to leave. Most cases are handled by post, email or phone once you’re in a plan.

H3: A Simple Script If Someone Knocks

“I’m handling this in writing. Please leave now.” Then contact us so we can help you formalise your budget and route. See Stop Debt Collectors for more tips on reducing contact pressure.

H3: Keep Notes

Record date/time and anything said. If you feel uncomfortable, end the conversation. We’ll help you move everything to writing.

Debt Solutions If Resolvecall Is Chasing You

Debt Management Plan (DMP)

Informal, one monthly payment across your non-priority debts. Many creditors freeze or reduce interest/charges when a fair budget is presented. Easier to adjust if your income changes.

Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA)

Legally binding and structured. A single affordable payment for a set period; at successful completion, remaining eligible unsecured debt may be written off. Often provides strong protection from most creditor action.

Debt Relief Order (DRO)

For low income/low assets. Debts are usually frozen for 12 months and then written off if your situation hasn’t improved. Strict eligibility applies—ask us to check.

Resolvecall (Resolve Call) FAQs

H3: Are Resolvecall bailiffs?

No—Resolvecall are debt collectors, not bailiffs. They cannot force entry or take goods without a court process that leads to enforcement agents. Most conversations happen by post or phone.

H3: Can Resolvecall visit my home?

They may send a field agent to knock and ask you to get in touch. You’re not obliged to let them in and can ask them to leave. If you prefer, handle everything in writing. We’ll help you set that up.

H3: Will they add interest or charges?

It depends on the account type and status. Many creditors reduce or freeze interest/charges when a fair arrangement is agreed—commonly via a DMP.

H3: I don’t recognise the debt—what should I do?

Request the key facts and run a free credit check with us to verify before paying anything. Accuracy first, payments second.

H3: Will a solution hurt my credit file?

Defaults/arrears and formal solutions like an IVA can affect your file. For many people, the priority is stabilising the household and stopping the spiral—then rebuilding credit in time.

H3: Can Resolvecall take me to court?

Collectors can recommend legal action, but many cases avoid court when you engage promptly and provide a defendable budget. If claim forms arrive, talk to us immediately and we’ll guide the response.

H3: How fast can you help after I submit the JotForm?

We aim to call you promptly (and WhatsApp if we can’t get through). We start fact-finding and budgeting right away, focusing on urgent risks like council tax and utilities.

H3: Do I need a complete list of debts first?

No. We’ll guide a free credit check and ask the right questions to catch any missing accounts.

H3: I’m self-employed—can you help?

Yes. We support sole traders and directors. See Business Debt and Tax/HMRC for extra guidance.
