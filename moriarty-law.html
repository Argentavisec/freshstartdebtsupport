Moriarty Law Debt Recovery – What You Need to Know

Received a letter, claim form, or County Court Judgment (CCJ) threat from Moriarty Law Solicitors? You’re not alone. This firm often acts on behalf of creditors like utility companies, mobile phone providers, and debt buyers. But before you panic — understand your rights and how to respond.

Fresh Start Debt Support helps people across the UK deal with companies like Moriarty Law. Whether you're worried about legal action or struggling to repay, we can help you understand your options without judgment or pressure.

Who Are Moriarty Law?

Moriarty Law is a UK-based legal firm that specialises in debt recovery and litigation. They are known for issuing pre-action letters and court claims on behalf of their clients, who may include:

  • Lowell Portfolio
  • Utility providers (e.g., gas, electricity, water)
  • Mobile networks
  • Other debt purchasers or creditors

If you've received contact from Moriarty Law, it's likely related to a debt that has either been sold to a third party or is in arrears. But just because you receive a letter, it doesn’t mean court action is guaranteed — and there may be ways to stop it.

What Actions Can Moriarty Law Take?

Moriarty Law may send:

  • Pre-legal letters requesting payment
  • Letters before claim (LBC)
  • County Court claims
  • Applications for CCJs if ignored

If a CCJ is issued and goes unpaid, enforcement methods like bailiffs, wage deductions, or charging orders may follow.

It's important to respond early — but that doesn’t always mean paying in full. We can help you explore legally recognised ways to stop debt collectors or reduce what you owe through affordable repayment plans.

How to Stop Action from Moriarty Law

You may be able to prevent further legal steps if you act early. Depending on your circumstances, you could:

  • Settle the debt (in full or at a reduced rate)
  • Agree a repayment plan
  • Include the debt in a formal solution like an IVA or DMP
  • Challenge the debt if it’s incorrect or unenforceable

Each situation is different. We don’t offer financial advice, but we can help you explore your options and check whether you're eligible for a government-backed or informal debt solution.

Can Moriarty Law Issue a CCJ?

Yes — Moriarty Law can issue County Court claims if a debt remains unpaid. Ignoring these letters can result in a CCJ by default, which can damage your credit score for six years.

Before that happens, you may be able to respond with a defence, propose a settlement, or include the debt in a plan like a Debt Relief Order or IVA. Acting fast is key to protecting your rights and credit file.

Include Moriarty Law Debts in a Formal Plan

Many people choose to include debts from firms like Moriarty Law in a wider solution, such as:

  • Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA) – freezes interest and can write off unaffordable debt after 5 years
  • Debt Management Plan (DMP) – informal monthly payment plan with creditor agreement
  • Debt Relief Order (DRO) – for low income, no asset cases (eligibility criteria apply)

We can help you check whether you qualify for one of these routes and what impact it may have on your finances.

What If the Debt Isn't Yours?

If you believe Moriarty Law has contacted you about a debt you don’t owe, you have the right to request:

  • Proof of the debt (debt validation)
  • Details of the original creditor
  • Information on any court claims filed against you

Do not ignore the letter — take action and get help confirming your options.
