Who Are Rossendales and Why Are They Contacting Me?

Search intent: ‚Äúwho are Rossendales‚Äù

Rossendales operate as enforcement agents (commonly called bailiffs). UK local authorities may use them to collect council tax arrears and parking/traffic penalties (PCNs). When arrears escalate‚Äîtypically after reminders and legal steps for council tax (a liability order) or when PCNs progress‚Äîyour council may instruct an enforcement firm such as Rossendales to recover the debt and associated enforcement-stage fees.

Common Reasons Rossendales Might Contact You

Enforcement agents are allowed to contact you, but they must follow rules. You do not have to agree to unaffordable payments at the door or over the phone. Rossendales Enforcement Stages & Fees (How It Usually Flows)

Local authority enforcement typically follows three broad stages: Compliance (you‚Äôre sent a Notice of Enforcement), Enforcement (an agent attends your address) and‚Äîif unresolved‚Äîpotential Sale/Removal. Rossendales usually send a Notice of Enforcement with a deadline to respond. If you set a realistic arrangement at this stage, you may avoid a doorstep visit. Keep proof of every message and payment. Never offer more than your budget allows, even if you feel under pressure.

An enforcement agent may attend your address. You can communicate through the door or in writing‚Äîyou don‚Äôt have to let them in for council tax or PCNs on a first visit. If arrangements fail and goods are taken under control, further action may be considered. Our goal is to avoid escalation by protecting essentials early and setting a defendable, affordable plan.

Typical Statutory Fees (England & Wales, 2025)

Under the Taking Control of Goods (Fees) Regulations 2014, common fees include a Compliance fee of ¬£75, an Enforcement fee of ¬£235 (plus 7.5% on balances over ¬£1,500), and a Sale/Removal fee of ¬£110 (plus the same percentage uplift).
