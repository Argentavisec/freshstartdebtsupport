What is a Debt Management Plan (DMP)? A Flexible Way to Get Debt-Free

A Debt Management Plan (DMP) is an informal agreement between you and your creditors to repay your debts at an affordable rate over time. Unlike formal insolvency solutions, a DMP offers flexibility and can be tailored to your financial situation, making it a popular choice for many struggling with multiple debts.

Debt Management Plan vs IVA and Bankruptcy: Which is Right for You?

Understanding the differences helps you make an informed choice:

  • DMP: Informal, flexible, no legal protection, relies on creditor goodwill, slower debt repayment
  • IVA: Formal insolvency, legally binding, freezes interest and bailiff action, possible debt write-off after 5-6 years
  • Bankruptcy: Formal insolvency with significant consequences, quick debt write-off, possible asset loss

We provide free advice to help you weigh these options based on your personal circumstances and financial goals.

Debts Included in a Debt Management Plan

A DMP can cover most unsecured debts such as:

  • Credit cards and store cards
  • Personal loans and overdrafts
  • Utility arrears and council tax debts
  • Payday loans and guarantor loans
  • Catalogues and store finance

Secured debts like mortgages or car finance are usually excluded but can be managed alongside a DMP.

Stopping Bailiffs and Legal Enforcement with a Debt Management Plan

One of the key advantages of a DMP is its ability to help stop aggressive creditor actions. While not legally binding like an IVA, many creditors agree to:

  • Pause bailiff visits and legal enforcement
  • Stop court proceedings while the plan is in place
  • Communicate only through your debt management provider

This practical protection reduces stress and gives you breathing space to manage repayments effectively.

How a Debt Management Plan Affects Your Credit Rating

A DMP is informal and does not appear as a formal insolvency on your credit report. However, as you are paying less than your full debt, creditors may mark accounts as 'managed' or 'in an arrangement,' which can affect your credit score.

Despite this, a DMP shows you are actively addressing your debts and can be preferable to missed payments or defaulting without a plan.
