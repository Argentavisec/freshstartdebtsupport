What Is a Debt Management Plan (DMP)?

A Debt Management Plan (DMP) is a flexible, informal way to repay unsecured debts at a pace you can afford. If you're overwhelmed with repayments on credit cards, loans, or store cards, a DMP can consolidate your debts into one lower monthly payment without the need for insolvency.

How Does a DMP Work?

When you set up a DMP, a debt specialist will review your income and living costs. Based on what you can afford, they’ll negotiate with your creditors to accept a lower payment. You make one monthly payment, which is distributed between all included creditors.

This informal agreement avoids court action, reduces stress, and helps you gradually clear your debts. Many creditors will freeze interest and stop chasing you, as long as you stick to the plan.

What Debts Can Be Included?

A DMP can cover most unsecured debts such as:

  • Credit cards
  • Overdrafts
  • Personal loans
  • Store cards
  • Payday loans
  • Catalogues and old bills

You can’t usually include secured debts like mortgages or car finance, but your specialist can help you prioritise those separately.

Will It Affect My Credit Score?

A DMP will usually impact your credit file, especially if you’re behind on payments. However, it shows you're taking steps to fix the problem. Long term, paying off your debts can actually improve your financial standing.

Trending questions like “does a DMP hurt your credit?” show that people want to understand the consequences. While your score may dip, the peace of mind and control you gain is often worth it.

Is a DMP Right for Me?

You may be eligible for a DMP if:

  • You have over £2,000 in unsecured debt
  • You’re struggling to meet monthly payments
  • You have a regular income
