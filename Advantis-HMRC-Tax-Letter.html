What Is an Advantis HMRC Text?

It’s a message claiming HMRC has asked Advantis to contact you about tax arrears. Genuine messages typically direct you to make contact. But scammers mimic this — which is why verification is essential.

Related pages: Debt collectors hub • HMRC debt help • Stop debt collectors contacting you

Is My Advantis HMRC Text Genuine?

Maybe. Advantis does contact people about HMRC debts. However, many texts are fraudulent. Treat every message as unverified until you confirm it using the steps below.

  • Genuine: references a known tax period/letter, details match official sites
  • Scam: odd links, threats of arrest, requests for banking/NI details by text
Follow the Safe Verification Steps

How to Verify an Advantis HMRC Text Safely (Step-by-Step)

  1. Do not click any link in the text and don’t call numbers shown in it.
  2. Sign in to your HMRC account via GOV.UK (type the address manually).
  3. Check for HMRC letters you’ve recently received and match references.
  4. Cross-check Advantis contact details using their official website (typed manually) or GOV.UK.
  5. Only call or pay using published numbers/channels you found yourself.
  6. Keep evidence — screenshots, the sender’s number, times/dates.

Common Scam Signs in “HMRC/Advantis” Texts

  • Shortened/strange links; domains that aren’t GOV.UK or known Advantis addresses
  • Demands for immediate payment or threats of arrest/imprisonment
  • Requests for card/bank/NI details within the text/chat
  • Poor grammar/spelling or unusual formatting

Rule of thumb: If the message pressures you to pay right now via a link, verify independently first.

Advantis Contact Numbers & Domains — How to Cross-Check

Type addresses manually into your browser and compare the phone numbers and web addresses you see against what’s shown in the text. If they don’t match, assume it’s unsafe.

Never rely on numbers or URLs in a text message. Start from GOV.UK and navigate from there.

Your Rights When Dealing with Advantis/HMRC Debt

  • Treated fairly — no harassment or misleading tactics
  • Right to details about the debt (amount, period, references)
  • Repayments must be affordable; you shouldn’t be pushed into hardship

How to Reduce or Stop Nuisance Contact

  • Ask for communication in writing only
  • Keep a log of dates/times/numbers
  • Send a written complaint if behaviour feels unfair
  • Escalate if unresolved — we’ll route it to the right body

If You Paid a Scam by Mistake — What to Do

  1. Contact your bank immediately and explain the situation.
  2. Report the scam to Action Fraud and HMRC’s phishing service.
  3. Change passwords and enable two-factor authentication.
  4. Gather evidence — screenshots, bank references, dates.

Advantis HMRC Text — Frequently Asked Questions

Can HMRC contact me by text?

Yes, HMRC and their agents sometimes use texts, but scams are common. Always verify independently before responding or paying.

How do I verify a text safely?

Sign in to your HMRC account via GOV.UK; cross-check any numbers/URLs with official pages; only call/paying via those channels.

Is it safe to pay through a link in a text?

No. Use your HMRC account or call a number you found yourself on GOV.UK or Advantis’ official site.

What if the message is genuine but I can’t pay?

Ask about Time to Pay or consider Breathing Space. We’ll check best-fit options quickly.

How do I complain about conduct or errors?

Complain in writing to the agency first. If unresolved, escalate to the appropriate body. We can help route it correctly.
