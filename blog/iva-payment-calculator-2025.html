How IVA Payments Are Really Calculated (Not Just a Percentage of Debt)

A common myth is that an IVA payment is based on ‚Äúx% of your debt‚Äù. In reality, it is based on your affordable monthly contribution after essential living costs, agreed with an Insolvency Practitioner (IP) and your creditors.

1. Your total monthly income

This includes wages, benefits, pensions and any regular contributions from a partner or other adults in the home. Variable income (like overtime or self-employed work) is usually averaged out so your IVA doesn‚Äôt rely on your best month.

2. Essential living costs & priority bills

Before any IVA payment is even discussed, an adviser will go through your budget:

  • Rent or mortgage
  • Council tax
  • Gas, electric & water
  • Food, clothing & toiletries
  • Travel to work/school, car costs & insurance
  • Childcare, school costs & basic family activities

The goal is to protect your household first, then see what‚Äôs genuinely left for unsecured debts. This is exactly how many top IVA calculator tools work behind the scenes ‚Äî they focus on disposable income, not the size of your debt alone.

3. Disposable income (what‚Äôs left after essentials)

Your disposable income is your income minus those essential costs. If that figure is negative, an IVA may not be the right route ‚Äî something like a DRO, token payments or a different solution might be better.

4. A safety buffer (so your IVA is sustainable)

In practice, most IPs don‚Äôt take every last pound of your spare income. There is often a small buffer for fluctuating costs, emergencies and one-off expenses. Your IVA payment is usually slightly lower than your disposable income so you have room to cope with real life.

5. IVA term (typically 5‚Äì6 years)

Most IVAs run for 5‚Äì6 years. Your monthly payment multiplied by the number of months gives the total amount you repay into the IVA. Fees are taken from this pot, and whatever remains is distributed to your creditors. Any included unsecured debt not repaid at successful completion is usually written off.

Quick rule of thumb for IVA payments

A rough ‚ÄúIVA payment calculator UK‚Äù rule is that your monthly IVA contribution is often close to your disposable income after a modest buffer. That‚Äôs why entering realistic income and outgoings into any online IVA calculator matters more than the exact debt figure.

Homeowners & Equity in an IVA

If you own a property, your equity (the value of the property minus your mortgage and secured loans) is taken into account when an IVA is proposed. This can affect both the term and how creditors view the offer, which is why some people look for an ‚ÄúIVA calculator with equity‚Äù.

What usually happens with equity

  • You won‚Äôt normally be forced to sell your home solely because of an IVA.
  • Your IP will assess how much equity there is and whether realistic remortgage options exist.
  • If remortgaging isn‚Äôt possible, many IVAs include a 12-month extension instead of equity release, subject to the IVA terms.

Equity rules are technical, and they can make a big difference to whether an IVA is right for you. If your home is at risk due to arrears, see Mortgage Arrears or Rent Arrears for priority advice, then return to this IVA calculator to see how your surplus income looks once your home is stabilised.

Who Usually Qualifies for an IVA in 2025?

There is no single rule that fits everyone, but an IVA is often considered where:

  • You have multiple unsecured debts (e.g., cards, loans, catalogues, overdrafts).
  • You‚Äôre struggling to keep up with contractual payments and arrears are building.
  • You have at least a modest amount of spare income after essentials each month.
  • You want a formal, legally binding plan with clear end point and potential write-off.

An IVA is unlikely to be right if:

  • Most of what you owe is secured (like mortgages or car HP) or certain priority debts only.
  • You have no realistic spare income at all after covering essentials.
  • Your situation is temporary and might improve quickly (a flexible DMP may work better).

A specialist will look at your debts, income, assets and household to decide if an IVA is appropriate, or if other routes such as a DRO, DMP or even bankruptcy are better. Use this IVA payment calculator as a gateway to that conversation, not the final word.

IVA vs DMP vs DRO ‚Äî Which One Fits Your Numbers?

Your figures from the IVA calculator are a good starting point to compare different routes. Many people who search for an ‚ÄúIVA calculator UK‚Äù also want to know if a DMP or DRO might work better.

Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA)

  • Formal, legally binding agreement supervised by an Insolvency Practitioner.
  • Typically lasts 5‚Äì6 years.
  • Strong protection from most creditor enforcement once approved.
  • Included unsecured debt that remains at successful completion is usually written off.

Debt Management Plan (DMP)

  • Informal arrangement managed by a DMP provider.
  • More flexible ‚Äî payments can sometimes be adjusted up or down more easily.
  • No guaranteed write-off date; you keep paying until debts are cleared or settled.
  • Interest and charges may be frozen or reduced, but this depends on creditors.

Debt Relief Order (DRO)

  • Formal insolvency option for people with low income, low assets and limited debt.
  • Debts are frozen for usually 12 months, then written off if your situation hasn‚Äôt improved.
  • Strict eligibility criteria apply (including limits on assets, vehicle value and surplus income).

IVA Calculator & Payment FAQs

Does the IVA calculator show my exact monthly payment?

No. It‚Äôs designed to give a rough guide only. Your exact payment can only be set after a full review of your income, spending, debts, and circumstances by an Insolvency Practitioner.

Why is my estimated IVA payment lower than my current minimum payments?

In an IVA, the focus is on what you can realistically afford after essentials, not what creditors are asking for individually. That‚Äôs why the calculator uses your spare income, not your existing contractual payments.

What if the calculator shows I have no spare income?

Don‚Äôt panic. It may simply mean an IVA isn‚Äôt the right tool. Other options ‚Äî such as a Debt Relief Order, a very low payment DMP or short-term token payments ‚Äî might suit you better. A specialist can help you check.

Will my IVA payment ever go up or down?

It can. Most IVAs include an annual review and a process called a variation if your circumstances change significantly. If your income drops or essential costs rise, your payment can sometimes be reduced with creditor agreement. If your income increases, your payment might rise too.

Can I overpay my IVA to finish early?

Some people are able to settle an IVA early by offering a lump sum (for example from a relative or a windfall). This has to be formally proposed and approved, and depends on your individual agreement and creditor views.

Will my debts definitely be written off at the end?

If your IVA completes successfully and all terms are met, included unsecured debts are usually written off. But there is no guaranteed percentage of write-off, and certain debts may be excluded from the start.

Is this IVA calculator suitable if I‚Äôm self-employed?

Yes, as a starting point. Use your average monthly income and realistic business and household costs. If your income varies a lot, an adviser may look at a longer period of trading to build a stable IVA payment that you can keep up.
