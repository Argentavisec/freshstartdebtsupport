Need Help with Payday Loan Debt?

Payday loans can seem like a quick fix, but they often lead to high-interest debt spirals. If you're stuck paying back more than you borrowed or juggling multiple payday loans, you're not alone — and there are solutions.

Why Are Payday Loans So Dangerous?

  • Extremely high interest rates (often over 1000% APR)
  • Short repayment periods with penalties for missing payments
  • Traps borrowers in a cycle of repeat borrowing

What Happens If I Can't Repay?

If you miss payments, lenders may add fees, sell the debt to collectors, or take legal action. This can hurt your credit score and make future borrowing difficult.

Can Payday Loan Interest Be Frozen?

Yes. If you enter a supported debt solution like an IVA or DMP, interest and charges can often be frozen — and repayments reduced to something affordable.

Can Payday Loan Debt Be Written Off?

Yes. With an IVA, you may write off a significant portion of your payday loan debt after making fixed monthly payments for 5–6 years.

What Are My Options?

  • IVA (Individual Voluntary Arrangement) – One affordable monthly payment, legal protection from lenders, write off unaffordable debt
  • DMP (Debt Management Plan) – Informal agreement to repay at your pace, may freeze interest
  • DRO (Debt Relief Order) – For people with low income and assets, write off debts after 12 months
