Breathing Space — pause most enforcement, freeze most interest

Why it’s the first step

Standard vs Mental Health Crisis route

In England & Wales, the Debt Respite Scheme gives up to 60 days of protection on qualifying debts. During that window, most enforcement is paused and most interest/fees are frozen while you set your plan. There’s also a Mental Health Crisis route that lasts for the treatment period plus 30 days. Use this window to build a defendable budget and choose DMP/IVA/DRO.

Breathing Space isn’t a payment holiday — keep making affordable payments where possible. We’ll prioritise rent/mortgage, council tax and utilities, and then share a fair amount across other creditors.

Stop payday lenders taking money — cancel the CPA

What is a CPA and why cancel it?

Your card issuer must help

Payday lenders often collect via a continuous payment authority (CPA) on your card. If funds are short, repeat attempts can hit essentials. You can cancel a CPA with your bank/card provider — even if the lender disagrees — and ask for written confirmation. Consider a safe basic account away from affected lenders to protect income. Then roll the debt into one fair plan.

Script for your bank:
“I’m cancelling the continuous payment authority to [lender] immediately. Please block further payments and confirm in writing today.”

Payday loan refunds — irresponsible lending complaints

When a complaint may be worth it

Evidence that helps

If loans were repeatedly rolled over, topped up, or obviously unaffordable, you may be able to complain. Where upheld, lenders may refund interest/charges and adjust balances. We’ll help you map the timeline, pull statements and submit a clear affordability complaint. If the lender doesn’t resolve it within eight weeks, you can escalate to the Financial Ombudsman Service.

Complaints/refunds run separate to your debt solution — we’ll keep you protected with Breathing Space and a plan while the complaint proceeds.

Which route works best with payday loans? (DMP · IVA · DRO)

One affordable payment, realistic expectations

How each route treats interest/fees

DMP: Informal and flexible; many lenders freeze/reduce interest when your budget is realistic (not guaranteed).
IVA: Formal, legally binding; interest/charges typically frozen, and remaining qualifying unsecured debt can be written off at completion.
DRO: For low income/low assets within set criteria; qualifying debts are written off after 12 months (the application fee has been removed).

Priority vs non-priority — protect your home & essentials first

Why this order matters

What to fund before offers

Payday loans are non-priority. Fund rent/mortgage, council tax, gas/electric, water and food first, then offer a fair share to other creditors through a plan. This reduces risk and makes lenders more likely to accept reduced payments. See our guides on council tax arrears and utility bills.

Payday loan rollovers & charges — know the limits

Rollovers capped, overall cost capped

Why repeated extensions are risky

UK rules limit payday loan rollovers and cap the total cost of high-cost short-term credit at 100% of the amount borrowed. Late fees are also capped and lenders must follow conduct rules. If you’ve been rolled over multiple times or struggled to repay, consider a complaint and switch to one affordable plan.

If you’ve had repeated rollovers or multiple CPA attempts, that’s a red flag — include this evidence in any affordability complaint.
