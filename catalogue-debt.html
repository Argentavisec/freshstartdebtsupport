What Is Catalogue Debt?

Catalogue debt happens when you borrow using a mail order or catalogue account but fail to repay the balance on time. These accounts often have high interest rates, and missing payments can cause fees and interest to accumulate rapidly.

Catalogue debt is unsecured, which means there is no collateral involved, but the debt collectors can still apply pressure and take legal action if payments are not made.

Reasons catalogue debt can become difficult to manage include:

  • Unexpected expenses causing missed payments
  • Using catalogue credit for everyday living costs
  • High interest rates causing rapid debt growth
  • Only making minimum payments

Who Are Catalogue Debt Collectors?

If you fall behind on catalogue payments, your debt may be passed to third-party debt collection agencies. Common collectors who work with catalogue debts include:

  • Lowell Financial Ltd
  • Moriarty Law

Can Catalogue Credit Companies Take You to Court?

Yes. If you fail to keep up repayments, catalogue credit providers or their debt collectors can apply to the courts for a County Court Judgment (CCJ). This is a legal order to repay the debt and can significantly damage your credit rating for up to six years.

Once a CCJ is granted, enforcement actions can follow, including bailiffs visiting your home, wage garnishment, or freezing your bank accounts. Acting early and seeking professional help can often prevent court proceedings altogether.

What Is an IVA and How Can It Help Your Catalogue Debt?

An Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA) is a formal, legally binding agreement where you repay what you can afford over 5 to 6 years. At the end of the IVA, any remaining unsecured catalogue debt is usually written off.
