Include Catalogue/BNPL in One Plan — IVA, DMP or DRO

IVA — Individual Voluntary Arrangement

For steady income with higher total debts (often £6,000+). One affordable payment for 5–6 years; once approved, most enforcement and interest are typically frozen. On successful completion, remaining eligible unsecured debt may be written off.

DMP — Debt Management Plan

Informal and flexible. One reduced payment based on what you can afford; many creditors freeze or reduce interest/charges when your budget is defendable.

DRO — Debt Relief Order

For low income/low assets within set limits. Debts included are frozen for 12 months and may be written off if circumstances don’t improve.

Catalogue Debt — FAQs

Can catalogue debt be written off?

Possibly — typically via an IVA after successful completion, or a DRO if you meet strict criteria.

Will a plan stop interest and charges?

In a DMP, many creditors freeze or reduce interest; in an IVA, interest is generally frozen once approved.
