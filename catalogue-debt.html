What Is Catalogue Debt?

Catalogue debt occurs when you purchase items from catalogue companies and fall behind on the repayments. These companies often offer credit accounts, but if you're unable to keep up, the interest can build rapidly — turning small balances into overwhelming debts.

Common catalogue firms include:

  • 📦 Very
  • 📦 Littlewoods
  • 📦 Studio
  • 📦 JD Williams
  • 📦 Freemans
  • 📦 Look Again

Catalogue arrears can affect your credit score and may eventually be passed to debt collection agencies or bailiffs if left unpaid.

Best Ways to Deal with Catalogue Debt

There are several proven ways to deal with catalogue arrears depending on your income, total debt, and personal circumstances:

Debt Management Plan (DMP)

Ideal if you have multiple debts but can make small monthly payments. We negotiate with creditors to reduce interest and combine debts into one manageable plan.

Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA)

If you owe over £6,000 and can afford £80/month or more, an IVA can write off unaffordable debt after 5 years and stop all legal action and interest.

Debt Relief Order (DRO)

For people on low income or benefits with less than £30,000 of debt. Catalogue debt may be included in a DRO, which can clear all debt after 12 months.

Breathing Space Scheme

Grants up to 60 days of protection from collectors while you get support — no fees, no harassment, and no interest added during the period.

Catalogue Debt Help: Frequently Asked Questions

Can you go to jail for catalogue debt?

No — catalogue debt is civil, not criminal. You can’t be sent to prison for owing money to catalogues.

Can I get help if I'm on benefits?

Yes. Many people on Universal Credit or similar receive help through a DRO or DMP.

What happens if I ignore catalogue debt?

Ignoring the debt can lead to defaults, CCJs, bailiff action, or even deductions from wages. But help is available before it gets that far.

Will my credit score be affected?

If you’re already missing payments, your credit file may already be impacted. Setting up a solution can prevent further damage and help rebuild in time.
