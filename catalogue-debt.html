Include Catalogue/BNPL in One Plan — IVA, DMP or DRO

IVA — Individual Voluntary Arrangement

For steady income with higher total debts (often £6,000+). One affordable payment for 5–6 years; once approved, most enforcement and interest are typically frozen. On successful completion, remaining eligible unsecured debt may be written off.

DMP — Debt Management Plan

Informal and flexible. One reduced payment based on what you can afford; many creditors freeze or reduce interest/charges when your budget is defendable.

DRO — Debt Relief Order

For low income/low assets within set limits. Debts included are frozen for 12 months and may be written off if circumstances don’t improve.

Catalogue CCJ, Attachment of Earnings & £1 Token Payments — What to Do

Already got a CCJ?

You still have options. You can ask the court to vary payments to an affordable amount or, if there’s a valid reason (e.g., papers never received), to set aside the judgment. See Stop Court Action for steps.

Attachment of Earnings (AEO)

If deductions are unaffordable, apply to vary or suspend the order with a realistic budget (Breathing Space/DMP/IVA can also help). Read our short guide under court action.

£1 Token payments

When budgets are under severe pressure, short-term token offers (e.g., £1 per creditor) can hold the line while you stabilise or set up a longer-term solution. Pair with Breathing Space if appropriate.

Why BNPL stacks up

Multiple small instalments across retailers can overlap. Missed BNPL payments can lead to fees and collections. Consolidating into one plan prevents “stacking” and helps you prioritise essentials first.
