What is a Debt Relief Order (DRO) in 2025?

A Debt Relief Order (DRO) is a government-backed debt solution designed for people with low income, minimal assets, and debts typically under £30,000. DROs offer legal protection from creditors and can lead to a full debt write-off after a 12-month period.

How Does a Debt Relief Order Work?

When you enter a DRO, your debts are legally frozen for 12 months, during which creditors cannot take enforcement action. If your financial situation doesn’t improve within that year, most or all qualifying debts are written off, giving you a fresh financial start.

Key features of a DRO include:

  • ✅ Freezing of debts and creditor contact
  • ✅ No court or bailiff action during the order
  • ✅ Debt write-off after 12 months if conditions are met
  • ✅ Legal protection without bankruptcy’s harsher consequences

Who Qualifies for a Debt Relief Order in 2025?

To qualify for a DRO, you generally must:

  • ✔ Have total debts under £30,000 (this threshold may be reviewed periodically)
  • ✔ Have monthly disposable income below a certain limit (usually around £75)
  • ✔ Own assets valued under £300 (excluding your home and essential items)
  • ✔ Not own a car valued over £1,000 (unless essential for work or disability)
  • ✔ Be resident in England or Wales

DRO Compared to Other Debt Solutions in 2025

It’s important to understand where a DRO fits in the range of debt solutions:

  • DRO: For low income, minimal assets, small debts, legal write-off after 12 months
  • DMP: Informal, flexible repayment plans for those with steady income
  • IVA: Formal, legally binding agreement with debt write-off after 5-6 years
  • Bankruptcy: Formal insolvency with quick discharge but serious implications

Debts Included in a Debt Relief Order

A DRO can cover most unsecured debts such as:

  • Credit card debts
  • Personal loans and overdrafts
  • Payday loans and high-cost short-term credit
  • Utility arrears and council tax debts
  • Catalogues and store cards
  • Benefit overpayments

Secured debts like mortgages or car finance, court fines, and student loans cannot be included in a DRO.

How Does a DRO Affect Your Credit Rating?

A DRO will be recorded on your credit file and can remain for six years from the date it is approved. While it indicates financial difficulty, it also shows you took responsible steps to resolve your debts.

Compared to bankruptcy or IVAs, a DRO may have less severe long-term credit implications, making it a suitable option for those with low income.

Debt Relief Order FAQs for 2025

Can I apply for a DRO more than once?

Typically, you cannot have more than one DRO in six years unless exceptional circumstances apply. Our advisors will explain your options.

What happens if my financial situation improves during the DRO period?

If your circumstances change, you must inform the official receiver. This may affect the DRO status.

Is a DRO available in Scotland or Northern Ireland?

No, DROs are only available in England and Wales. Scotland has Trust Deeds, and Northern Ireland has separate insolvency procedures.

Are there any fees to apply for a DRO?

There is a small fee to apply (around £90), but Fresh Start Debt Support offers guidance on managing this and other costs.

Will creditors contact me during the DRO period?

No, creditors must stop contacting you for the duration of the DRO, providing essential relief from pressure.
